# Mushroom and Ricotta Stuffed Shells
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom and Ricotta Stuffed Shells have been around for centuries as a delicious Italian dish. Although its exact origin is unknown, it is believed to have originated in the south of Italy. Traditionally, the dish consists of large pasta shells stuffed with a mixture of ricotta cheese and sautéed mushrooms. To make it even more delicious, the shells are then smothered in a tomato sauce and baked until the cheese is golden and bubbly.

# Recipe Overview

. 
    
    The Prep Time for Mushroom and ricotta stuffed shells is 10 minutes. The Cooking Time is 25 minutes. The Skill Level is Easy and the Spice Level Scoville rating is 0.
    
    You will need a large pot, a 9×13 inch baking dish, a small saucepan, a wooden spoon, a strainer, and a measuring cup to prepare Mushroom and ricotta stuffed shells.

# Ingredients

- 12 jumbo pasta shells
    - 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
    - 1 cup chopped onion
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 8 ounces mushrooms, chopped
    - 1 teaspoon chopped fresh thyme
    - 1/2 teaspoon salt
    - 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
    - 2 cups ricotta cheese
    - 2 cups marinara sauce
    - 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
    - 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Ricotta Stuffed Shells Instructions 
    1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
    2. Grease a 9 x 13 inch baking dish with a tablespoon of olive oil.
    3. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and add 1 teaspoon of salt and the jumbo shells. Cook for 8 minutes, stirring occasionally to make sure the shells do not stick together.
    3. Drain the cooked shells and set them aside.
    4. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add 2 tablespoons of olive oil and mushrooms to the pan. Season with salt and pepper. Cook the mushrooms for 4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they are softened and lightly browned.
    5. Turn the heat down to medium-low and add the garlic, oregano, basil, red pepper flakes and tomato paste to the pan. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the garlic is fragrant.
    6. Add the crushed tomatoes and stir to combine. Simmer for 5 minutes.
    7. In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta, Parmesan, parsley and egg.
    8. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the spinach until it is wilted.
    9. Spread a thin layer of the tomato sauce in the bottom of the prepared baking dish.
    10. Stuff each shell with a heaping tablespoon of the ricotta mixture and place in the baking dish.
    11. Top the shells with the remaining sauce and sprinkle with mozzarella cheese.
    12. Bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes.
    13. Let cool for 10 minutes before serving. Enjoy!
