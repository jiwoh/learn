# Mushroom Gravy
    
    Mushroom gravy is a savory and creamy condiment, made with mushrooms and a variety of other ingredients such as butter, stock, flour and spices. It is commonly a topping for mashed potatoes, steamed vegetables, and other proteins like beef, chicken and pork.
    
    ## Origin and History of Mushroom Gravy 
    
    Mushroom gravy dates back to the late 1800s, when it was a popular accompaniment to all sorts of dishes. The French garlic sauce known as 'sauce aux champignons' was popularized in the early 1900s, and it is believed that this is the foundation of modern mushroom gravy. In the United States, mushroom gravy has been a common dish since the 1950s, and is still commonly served today.

# Ingredients

## Mushroom Gravy Ingredients
    * 2 tablespoons butter
    * 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
    * 2 cups vegetable broth
    * 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
    * 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    * 1 teaspoon garlic powder 
    * Salt and pepper, to taste

# Instructions

&lt;h2&gt;Mushroom Gravy Instructions&lt;/h2&gt;
    
    1. Heat 2 tablespoons of butter in a medium saucepan over medium-high heat.
    
    2. Add 2 cloves of minced garlic, 1/2 cup chopped mushrooms and 1/4 teaspoon of salt to the butter and cook until the mushrooms are softened, about 3 minutes.
    
    3. Pour 2 tablespoons of flour over the mushroom mixture and stir until the mixture thickens, about 1 minute.
    
    4. Slowly pour 1 cup of beef stock into the mixture, stirring constantly.
    
    5. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce is thickened.
    
    6. Reduce the heat to low and stir in 2 tablespoons of heavy cream.
    
    7. Taste and season with salt and pepper as desired.
