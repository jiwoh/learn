# Mushroom Parmesan
    
    Mushroom Parmesan is a popular Italian dish that combines mushrooms and cheese and is served over pasta. It is a rich and flavorful dish that is also easy to make at home.
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom Parmesan is believed to have originated in the late 19th century in Italy. It is an Italian comfort food that has been popularized worldwide. The dish combines Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, mushrooms, olive oil, and garlic, to create a rich and creamy sauce. It is often served over pasta, such as spaghetti and can be topped with parsley.

# Ingredients

###### Mushroom Parmesan Ingredients
    - 4 to 6 large portobello mushrooms, wiped clean
    - 1/4 cup olive oil
    - 2 teaspoons Italian seasoning
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
    - 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, freshly grated
    - Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
    - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley leaves

# Instructions

## Mushroom Parmesan Instructions

    2. Grease a baking dish with butter or oil.
    3. Place mushrooms in the prepared baking dish.
    4. Drizzle mushrooms with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
    5. Roast in preheated oven for 15-20 minutes.
    6. Remove baking dish from oven and sprinkle mushrooms with Parmesan cheese.
    7. Bake for an additional 5 minutes or until cheese is melted and bubbly.
    8. Serve warm. Enjoy!
