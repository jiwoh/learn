# Mushroom and Wild Rice Soup
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom and Wild Rice Soup is a classic dish originating in the 1950s in the Midwest region of the United States. A combination of mushrooms, wild rice, butter, and cream, this creamy soup has been a favorite of many home cooks for decades.

# Recipe Overview

    The prep time for Mushroom and Wild Rice Soup is 15 minutes, the cooking time is 20-25 minutes, the skill level is easy and the spice level Scoville rating is 0. To prepare Mushroom and Wild Rice Soup, you will need a chopping knife, cutting board, large pot, measuring spoons, measuring cups, and soup ladle.

# Ingredients

**Ingredients for Mushroom and Wild Rice Soup:**
    - 2 tablespoons butter
    - 2 shallots, chopped
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1 (8 ounce) package button mushrooms, sliced
    - 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
    - 5 cups chicken broth
    - 2/3 cup wild rice
    - 2 sprigs fresh thyme
    - 1 bay leaf
    - 1/2 teaspoon salt
    - 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
    - 1/2 cup heavy cream

# Instructions

### Instructions
    
    1. Heat a large pot over medium-high heat and add 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
    2. Add 1 sliced onion and cook until softened, about 3 minutes.
    3. Add 1 pound of sliced mushrooms and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    4. Add 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley, 1 teaspoon of minced garlic, and 1 teaspoon of dried thyme. Cook for an additional 2 minutes.
    5. Pour in 6 cups of vegetable broth and stir in 1 cup of wild rice.
    6. Increase the heat to high and bring to a boil.
    7. Reduce the heat to low and simmer, partially covered, for 40 minutes, or until the rice is tender.
    8. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
    9. Serve hot. Enjoy!
