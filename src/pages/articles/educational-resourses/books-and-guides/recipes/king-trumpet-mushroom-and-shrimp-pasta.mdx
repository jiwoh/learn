# Ingredients

- 12 oz. linguini
    - 1 lb. large shrimp, peeled and deveined
    - 1 cup (4 oz.) sliced king trumpet mushrooms
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
    - 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
    - 2 tablespoons butter
    - 1/4 cup finely grated Parmesan cheese
    - 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
    - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
    - Salt and ground black pepper, to taste

# Instructions

## King Trumpet Mushroom and Shrimp Pasta Instructions
    1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil over high heat.
    2. Add 12 ounces of dried spaghetti and cook until al dente according to package instructions.
    3. Meanwhile, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
    4. Add 1 pound of peeled and deveined shrimp and cook until opaque, about 3 minutes.
    5. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside.
    6. To the same skillet add 2 cloves of minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
    7. Add 2 diced shallots and cook until softened, about 3 minutes.
    8. Add 1 pound of trimmed and sliced King Trumpet mushrooms and cook until softened, about 4 minutes.
    9. Add the cooked shrimp back to the skillet and season with 1 teaspoon of kosher salt and 1/2 teaspoon of freshly cracked black pepper.
    10. Pour in 1/2 cup of dry white wine and simmer until slightly reduced, about 2 minutes.
    11. Add 1/2 cup of heavy cream and bring to a simmer.
    12. Add the cooked spaghetti to the skillet along with 2 tablespoons of minced parsley.
    13. Toss to evenly combine and cook until the sauce thickens and coats the pasta, about 2 minutes.
    14. Serve with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and additional parsley if desired. Enjoy!
