# Mushroom and Cheese Strata
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom and cheese strata is a classic Italian dish that has been enjoyed for centuries. It is a layered dish made of bread or pasta, cheese, and mushrooms cooked in a rich, creamy custard-like sauce. This hearty meal has been popularized throughout Italy, and is now a popular option for brunch, lunch, or dinner menus around the world.

# Ingredients

**Ingredients**
    
    - 6 cups cubed day-old French bread
    - 4 cups diced fresh mushrooms
    - 1 onion, chopped
    - 1/4 cup butter
    - 3 cups shredded sharp Cheddar cheese
    - 4 eggs
    - 2 cups milk
    - 1 teaspoon salt
    - 1/4 teaspoon pepper
    - 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme
    - 2 tablespoons minced fresh parsley

# Instructions

## Mushroom and cheese strata Instructions
    1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
    2. Grease a 9x13-inch (23x33-cm) baking dish.
    3. Spread one package of frozen, chopped spinach on bottom of the dish.
    4. Top with one 16-ounce (450-gram) package of sliced mushrooms, 1/2 cup (4 ounces or 115 grams) sliced yellow onion, 1/4 teaspoon (1 gram) garlic powder, 1/2 teaspoon (2.5 mL) dried oregano, and 2 tablespoons (25 grams) butter.
    5. Sprinkle 1/4 cup (25 grams) grated Parmesan cheese on top.
    6. Beat 6 eggs and 1 1/2 cups (360 mL) milk together in a medium-sized bowl.
    7. Pour egg mixture over vegetables in the baking dish.
    8. Top with 12 slices of buttered French bread and 1 cup (4 ounces or 115 grams) shredded Swiss cheese.
    9. Bake for 40 minutes or until golden brown.
    10. Let stand before serving.
