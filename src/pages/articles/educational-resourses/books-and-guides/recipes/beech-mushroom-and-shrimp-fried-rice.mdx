# Beech Mushroom and Shrimp Fried Rice
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Beech mushrooms are a specialty of Japanese cuisine, often served as a savory side dish. Shrimp fried rice is a Chinese style fried rice dish, featuring shrimp as one of the main ingredients. The combination of these two ingredients to create Beech Mushroom and Shrimp Fried Rice is a relatively modern version of this classic dish, combining the best of both cuisines.

# Recipe Overview

    **Prep Time:** 10 minutes  
    **Cooking Time:** 15 minutes  
    **Skill Level:** Beginner  
    **Spice Level Scoville rating:** Mild  
    
    To prepare Beech Mushroom and Shrimp Fried Rice, you will need a wok, a cutting board, a knife, a spatula, and a bowl.

# Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    - 3 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1 teaspoon grated ginger
    - 10 ounces beech mushrooms, stems removed
    - 8 ounces shrimp, peeled and deveined
    - 4 scallions, finely chopped
    - 4 cups cooked white rice
    - 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    - 2 tablespoons sesame oil
    - Salt and pepper to taste

# Instructions

## Beech Mushroom and Shrimp Fried Rice Instructions
    
    1. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
    2. Add 1 cup of diced beech mushrooms and 1 pound of medium shrimp, peeled and deveined. Cook, stirring often until the shrimp is cooked through, about 10 minutes.
    3. Add 2 cups of cooked white rice, 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 1⁄4 teaspoon Chinese five-spice powder, 1 teaspoon of sesame oil, 1 tablespoon of rice wine vinegar, and 1 teaspoon of minced fresh ginger. Stir fry for about 5 minutes.
    4. Add 1 chopped emerald onion and 1⁄4 cup of frozen peas. Stir fry for an additional 3 minutes.
    5. Serve warm, topped with chopped emerald onion.
