# Shiitake Mushroom and Egg Fried Rice
    
    ## Origin and History
    Shiitake mushroom and egg fried rice is a traditional Chinese dish that is believed to have originated in the Hunan province of China. It is a simple dish made with only a few ingredients including shiitake mushrooms, eggs, cooked white rice, and a few seasonings and condiments. Over time, variations of the dish have been created using different types of mushrooms or other ingredients, such as cashews or shredded pork. This dish is a staple in Chinese cuisine and is enjoyed all over the world.

# Recipe Overview

    *Prep Time: 20 mins 
    *Cooking Time: 15 mins
    *Skill Level: Easy

# Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1/2 cup diced onion
    - 1/2 cup diced bell pepper
    - 2 cups fresh shiitake mushrooms, stems removed and diced
    - 1 cup cooked white rice
    - 2 eggs, beaten
    - 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    - 2 tablespoons sesame oil
    - 2 emerald onions, sliced

# Instructions

###Ingredients: 
    - 2 cups cooked rice
    - 5-6 shiitake mushrooms 
    - 2 eggs
    - 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
    - 1 teaspoon sesame oil
    - 2 tablespoons chopped scallions 
    - 2 tablespoons soy sauce 
    - Salt and pepper to taste
    
    ###Instructions: 
    1. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
    2. Add the mushrooms and cook for about 2 minutes, until tender.
    3. Crack the eggs into the pan, and stir until lightly scrambled.
    4. Add the cooked rice and stir to combine.
    5. Add the sesame oil, soy sauce, salt, and pepper and stir.
    6. Reduce the heat to low and let the fried rice cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    7. Add the scallions and stir to combine.
    8. Taste and adjust the seasonings, if needed.
    9. Serve warm. Enjoy!
