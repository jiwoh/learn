  # Introduction

## Introduction
    Portobello Mushroom and Spinach Quesadillas are a delicious and creative Mexican spin on the classic quesadilla. With a perfectly balanced mix of mushrooms and spinach, these quesadillas are a flavorful, filling and healthy meal. 
    
    ## Origin and History 
    The portobello mushroom is a variety of the common mushroom (Agaricus bisporus) that is found worldwide. The portobello mushroom first appeared in the early 1990s, and has since been embraced by chefs for its large size and meaty texture. 
    
    The combination of portobello mushrooms and spinach in quesadillas, is a relatively recent invention, first appearing in Mexican cooking in the late 2000s. The flavor pairing quickly caught on and has become a staple dish at Mexican restaurants and households across the country.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Portobello Mushroom and Spinach Quesadillas is 15 minutes, the Cooking Time is about 20 minutes, the Skill Level required is Easy, and the Spice Level Scoville rating is 0. In order to prepare Portobello Mushroom and Spinach Quesadillas, you will need a skillet, a cutting board, a knife, a plate, and a spatula.

# Ingredients

## Ingredients
    
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1 small onion, chopped 
    - 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil 
    - 2 large portobello mushrooms, cleaned and diced 
    - 2 cups fresh baby spinach leaves 
    - 8 (8-inch) whole wheat tortillas 
    - 1 cup (4 ounces) crumbled feta cheese 
    - 1/4 cup (1 ounce) grated Monterey Jack cheese 
    - Nonstick cooking spray

# Instructions

&lt;h2&gt;Portobello Mushroom and Spinach Quesadillas Instructions&lt;/h2&gt;
    
    1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). 
    2. Heat 1 tablespoon (14 g) of oil in a medium-sized skillet over medium-high heat. 
    3. Add 2 portobello mushrooms, sliced, and stir-fry until they're tender and starting to brown, about 4 to 5 minutes. 
    4. Add 2 cups (60 g) of fresh spinach and cook for another minute until wilted. 
    5. Remove vegetables from the skillet and set aside. 
    6. Heat another tablespoon (14 g) of oil in the skillet over medium heat.
    7. Place 2 large whole wheat tortillas in the skillet and cook for about 2 minutes until lightly browned. 
    8. Flip the tortillas over, and spread the cooked mushrooms and spinach evenly over one side. 
    9. Sprinkle 1/4 cup (20 g) of grated cheese over the vegetables. 
    10. Place the second tortilla on top.
    11. Cook for another 2 minutes or until the bottom is lightly browned. 
    12. Carefully flip the quesadilla, and cook for another 3 minutes or until the other side is lightly browned. 
    13. Transfer the quesadilla to a baking sheet, and bake in the preheated oven for 10 minutes or until the cheese is melted. 
    14. Serve warm. Enjoy!
