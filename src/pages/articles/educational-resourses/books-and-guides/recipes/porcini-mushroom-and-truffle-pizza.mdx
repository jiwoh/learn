# Porcini Mushroom and Truffle Pizza
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    The combination of porcini mushrooms and truffles is a classic Italian culinary combination. Porcini mushrooms have been used in Italian cuisine for centuries, while truffles first began to appear in the early 20th century. This combination of earthy, savoury flavours is a popular choice for pizza, as the rich and meaty flavours pair perfectly with the bright and acidic tomato sauce. Porcini mushrooms and truffle pizza has become an iconic dish in Italy and is now enjoyed around the world.

# Recipe Overview

The Prep Time for Porcini Mushroom and Truffle Pizza is approximately 30 minutes, the Cooking Time is approximately 20 minutes, the Skill Level is Moderate, and the Spice Level Scoville rating is 0. To prepare this delicious dish, you will need a baking dish, bowl, knife, cutting board, spoon, and a stove.

# Ingredients

### Porcini Mushroom and Truffle Pizza Ingredients
    
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - 1 package (8 ounces) fresh porcini mushrooms, thickly sliced
    - 2 tablespoons truffle oil
    - 1 teaspoon dried oregano
    - 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
    - Salt and freshly ground black pepper
    - 4 ounces mozzarella cheese, cut into small cubes
    - 4 ounces Parmesan cheese, grated
    - 1/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese for garnish
    - 1/4 cup fresh parsley leaves for garnish

# Instructions

## Porcini Mushroom and Truffle Pizza Instructions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. In a large bowl, mix together 1 pound of prepared pizza dough, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 1/4 teaspoon of sea salt and 1/4 teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper. Knead the dough in the bowl for 3-4 minutes or until it is well mixed.
    3. Grease a 12-inch pizza pan with 1 tablespoon of olive oil.
    4. Place the dough on the pizza pan, and using your fingers, press down and outwards to form an even, circular shape.
    5. Bake the pizza dough in the preheated oven for 10 minutes.
    6. Meanwhile, in a medium skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat.
    7. Add 1/2 cup of sliced porcini mushrooms and sauté for 2 minutes.
    8. Add 1/4 cup of diced onion and 1 minced garlic clove, and sauté for 3 minutes.
    9. Add 1 tablespoon of freshly chopped thyme leaves and 1/2 teaspoon of truffle salt.
    10. Sauté for 1 minute, then remove from the heat and set aside.
    11. Spread the mushroom mixture evenly over the pre-baked pizza crust.
    12. Sprinkle 1/2 cup of grated mozzarella cheese over the mushroom layer.
    13. Bake the pizza in the preheated oven for 8-10 minutes or until the cheese is melted and the edges of the pizza are lightly golden brown.
    14. Remove from the oven and allow the pizza to cool for 5 minutes before slicing.
    15. Sprinkle with freshly chopped parsley and freshly grated truffle salt before serving. Enjoy!
