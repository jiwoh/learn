# Maitake Mushroom and Parmesan Polenta
    Maitake Mushroom and Parmesan Polenta is a savory and creamy dish that celebrates the unique flavor of maitake mushrooms and the nutty depth of Parmesan cheese. This hearty and comforting side dish is perfect for any night of the week.
    
    ## Origin and History
    Maitake mushrooms have been appreciated in Japan for centuries, revered for their flavor and medicinal properties. As for the Parmesan cheese, it is said to have originated in Italy in the 12th century. The combination of the two ingredients has been a favorite Italian side dish since the 16th century.

# Recipe Overview

The Prep Time for Maitake Mushroom and Parmesan Polenta is 15 minutes, the Cooking Time for Maitake Mushroom and Parmesan Polenta is 25 minutes, the Skill Level for Maitake Mushroom and Parmesan Polenta is intermediate, and the Spice Level Scoville rating for Maitake Mushroom and Parmesan Polenta is 0. You will need a pot, a large skillet, a wooden spoon, and a large bowl to prepare Maitake Mushroom and Parmesan Polenta.

# Ingredients

- 1 cup polenta
    - 4 cups vegetable broth
    - 3 tablespoons butter
    - 1 tablespoon olive oil 
    - 1 onion, finely chopped
    - 1 clove garlic, minced
    - 8 ounces Maitake mushrooms, coarsely chopped 
    - Salt and pepper
    - 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated

# Instructions

## Maitake Mushroom and Parmesan Polenta Instructions
    1. Bring 2 cups of salted water to a boil in a medium saucepan.
    2. Add 1 cup of polenta and reduce the heat to low.
    3. Cook, stirring often, for about 10 minutes, or until the polenta is thick and let it cool.
    4. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
    5. Add 1 minced garlic clove and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
    6. Add 1/2 pound of maitake mushrooms and cook until they are softened and lightly browned, about 5 minutes.
    7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
    8. Add 1/4 cup of white wine and cook until the liquid is evaporated, about 2 minutes.
    9. Stir in 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese and the cooled polenta.
    10. Cook until heated through, about 3 minutes.
    11. Serve warm with additional Parmesan cheese if desired.
