# Mushroom and Chorizo Paella
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom and chorizo paella is a popular Spanish dish which combines the flavors of mushrooms and chorizo, a Spanish type of sausage. This combination has been a favorite of Spanish cooks since the Roman times. The modern version of paella was developed in the 19th century in Valencia, Spain and typically includes rice, vegetables, and meat such as chicken and rabbit. The combination of mushrooms and chorizo has become popular in the last few decades and has spread around the world.

# Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons olive oil 
    - 250g of chorizo
    - 1 onion, finely chopped 
    - 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped 
    - 250g paella or risotto rice 
    - 1 litre hot chicken or vegetable stock 
    - 200g mushrooms, chopped into quarters 
    - 1 teaspoon smoked paprika 
    - 2 red peppers, de-seeded and sliced 
    - 2 tomatoes, deseeded and chopped 
    - Handful of fresh parsley, chopped 
    - 200g prawns
