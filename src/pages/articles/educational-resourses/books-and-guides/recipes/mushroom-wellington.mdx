# Mushroom Wellington
    
    Mushroom Wellington is a delicious main course dish consisting of mushrooms, cheese, and a puff pastry casing. This dish is a great way to cook mushrooms to tender perfection and make them the star of the meal. Originating in England in the mid-19th century, Mushroom Wellington is a classic comfort food dish that is sure to impress.

# Recipe Overview

    #### Prep Time for Mushroom Wellington: 25 minutes
    #### Cooking Time for Mushroom Wellington: 45 minutes
    #### Skill Level for Mushroom Wellington: Intermediate
    #### Spice Level Scoville rating for Mushroom Wellington: No Spice
    
    Utensils, pots, pans and other necessary items needed for preparing Mushroom Wellington include a rolling pin, cutting board, large bowl, skillet, parchment paper, and baking sheet.

# Ingredients

## Mushroom Wellington Ingredients
    - Puff Pastry

    - Fresh thyme leaves 
    - Salt and pepper 
    - Egg (for egg wash)

# Instructions

## Mushroom Wellington Instructions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
    
    2. Heat a sauté pan over medium heat and add 1/2 teaspoon of oil.
    
    3. Add 1/2 cup of diced shallots and sauté until they are softened and lightly browned, about 4-5 minutes.
    
    4. Add the 12 ounces of diced mushrooms to the pan and cook until the liquid from the mushrooms has cooked off and the mushrooms begin to brown, about 10-15 minutes.
    
    5. Add 1 tablespoon of fresh chopped thyme, 1 tablespoon of fresh chopped sage, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt. Cook for 1 minute.
    
    6. Remove from heat and transfer the mushroom mixture to a bowl to cool.
    
    7. Place the puff pastry sheet on a lightly floured surface and cut into 4 equal rectangles.
    
    8. Place 2 tablespoons of the cooled mushroom mixture onto one side of each puff pastry rectangle.
    
    9. Brush the edges of the pastry with the egg wash.
    
    10. Fold the pastry over to form a pocket, pressing the edges to seal.
    
    11. Place the 4 prepared puff pastry pockets onto a parchment-lined baking sheet.
    
    12. Brush the tops of the pockets with the egg wash.
    
    13. Bake in preheated oven for 20-25 minutes until the pastry is golden brown.
    
    14. Serve warm with a side of your choice. Enjoy!
