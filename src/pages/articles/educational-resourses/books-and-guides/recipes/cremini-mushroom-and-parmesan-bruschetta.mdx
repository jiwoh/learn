# Cremini Mushroom and Parmesan Bruschetta
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Cremini Mushroom and Parmesan Bruschetta is an Italian appetizer inspired by the traditional Italian bruschetta. It combines cremini mushrooms, Parmesan cheese, garlic, and basil for a delicious and flavorful dish. It was likely first served in the late 1800s in the Tuscany region, and has since become a popular appetizer all around the world.

# Recipe Overview

The Prep Time for Cremini Mushroom and Parmesan Bruschetta is 15 minutes, the Cooking Time is 20 minutes, the Skill Level is easy, and the Spice Level Scoville rating is 0. To prepare Cremini Mushroom and Parmesan Bruschetta, you will need a cutting board, a chef's knife, a large sauté pan, and a baking sheet.

# Ingredients

- 1 pint Cremini mushrooms
    - 1/4 cup shredded Parmesan cheese
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1/4 teaspoon thyme
    - salt and pepper, to taste
    - 4 slices of Italian bread, toasted
    - 1 tablespoon chopped parsley (optional)

# Instructions

## Cremini Mushroom and Parmesan Bruschetta Instructions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
    
    2. Place 8 slices of french bread on a baking sheet and lightly toast for about 5 minutes.
    
    3. Combine 1/2 cup of freshly grated Parmesan cheese with 1/4 cup of extra virgin olive oil. 
    
    4. Spread the Parmesan oil mixture over the french bread slices.
    
    5. In a large skillet over medium heat, heat 1 tablespoon of extra virgin olive oil. 
    
    6. Add 2 cups of freshly chopped cremini mushrooms and 1 teaspoon of minced garlic, cooking until the mushrooms are tender and lightly browned, about 8 minutes. 
    
    7. Season the mushrooms with 1/2 teaspoon of sea salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste. Stir in 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley and set aside to cool.
    
    8. Once the French bread slices are toasted, top with the cooled cremini mushrooms.
    
    9. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 10 minutes until the cheese is melted and the bruschetta is lightly browned.
    
    10. Serve hot and enjoy!
