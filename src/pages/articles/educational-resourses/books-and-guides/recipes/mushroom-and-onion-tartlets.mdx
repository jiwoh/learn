# Mushroom and Onion Tartlets
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom and onion tartlets are miniature tarts filled with mushrooms, onions, and other ingredients, such as cheese and herbs, and then baked until golden brown. This savory pastry dish has origins in French cuisine and is popular around the world. The tartlets are commonly served as an appetizer, but can also be enjoyed as a main course.

# Recipe Overview

Prep Time for Mushroom and onion tartlets: 20 minutes
    Cooking Time for Mushroom and onion tartlets: 25 minutes
    Skill Level for Mushroom and onion tartlets: Easy
    Spice Level Scoville rating for Mushroom and onion tartlets: Mild
    
    To prepare Mushroom and onion tartlets, you will need a muffin tin, knife, chopping board, frying pan, and bowl.

# Ingredients

- Puff pastry sheets 
    - 2 tbsp olive oil
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 2 cups mushrooms, sliced
    - 1 onion, diced
    - 2 sprigs parsley, finely chopped
    - Salt and pepper, to taste
    - 2 eggs, beaten
    - 1/2 cup shredded cheese

# Instructions

## Mushroom and onion tartlets Instructions
    
    1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
    2. Grease a 12-cup tartlet pan with butter or margarine.
    3. In a large bowl, mix together 1/2 cup (125 mL) chopped onion, 1/2 cup (125 mL) chopped mushrooms, and 1/2 cup (125 mL) shredded cheese.
    4. In a separate bowl, whisk 1 large egg, 2 Tbsp (30 mL) milk, 1/4 tsp (1 mL) salt and 1/4 tsp (1 mL) pepper.
    5. Add the egg mixture to the onion, mushroom and cheese mixture.
    6. Divide the mixture evenly among the 12 tartlet cups.
    7. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the tartlets are golden brown.
    8. Serve warm. Enjoy!
