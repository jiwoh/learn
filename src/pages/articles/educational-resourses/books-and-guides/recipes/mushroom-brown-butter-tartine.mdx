# Mushroom Brown Butter Tartine
    
    Originating in France, Mushroom Brown Butter Tartine is a classic French open-face sandwich that is characterized by thickly sliced bread (typically baguette or sourdough) that is lightly toasted and topped with creamy butter and mushrooms sautéed in butter. It is an elegant and sophisticated dish that can be served as an entrée or as a starter for any meal.

# Recipe Overview

    **Prep Time for Mushroom Brown Butter Tartine**: 20 minutes 
    **Cooking Time for Mushroom Brown Butter Tartine**: 15 minutes 
    **Skill Level for Mushroom Brown Butter Tartine**: Easy 
    **Spice Level Scoville Rating for Mushroom Brown Butter Tartine**: 0
    
    To prepare Mushroom Brown Butter Tartine, you will need a large skillet, a knife, a cutting board, butter, and mushrooms.

# Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 8 ounces cremini mushrooms, sliced
    - 2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves
    - ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
    - ¼ teaspoon sea salt
    - 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
    - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
    - 4 slices of rustic bread
    - 4 ounces Brie cheese

# Instructions

# Mushroom Brown Butter Tartine Instructions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
    
    2. Spread the mushrooms onto a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt. Roast in preheated oven for 15 minutes.
    
    3. Heat the butter in a medium skillet over medium heat.
    
    4. Once the butter starts to foam, reduce the heat to low and cook, stirring occasionally, until the butter turns a golden brown color, about 5 minutes.
    
    5. Take the skillet off the heat and add the roasted mushrooms.
    
    6. Toast the bread slices.
    
    7. Spread the brown butter mushrooms onto the toast.
    
    8. Sprinkle with freshly grated Parmesan cheese.
    
    9. Serve immediately. Enjoy!
