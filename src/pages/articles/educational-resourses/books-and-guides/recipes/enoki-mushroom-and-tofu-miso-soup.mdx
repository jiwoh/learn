# Enoki Mushroom and Tofu Miso Soup
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Enoki Mushroom and Tofu Miso Soup is a traditional Japanese soup made from enoki mushrooms, soft tofu, and miso paste. It is easy to make and is perfect for a light lunch or dinner. The dish dates back to the Edo Period (1603-1868) when miso soup with enoki mushrooms began to appear in writings. After the introduction of tofu in the Meiji period (1868 - 1912), tofu was added to the soup base, creating the popular dish it is today.

# Ingredients

### Ingredients:
    - 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 2 tablespoons ginger, minced
    - 5 cups vegetable broth
    - 2 tablespoons miso paste
    - 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    - 1/2 teaspoon black pepper 
    - 1 package enoki mushrooms 
    - 8 ounces firm tofu, diced 
    - 2 emerald onions, sliced

# Instructions

## Enoki Mushroom and Tofu Miso Soup Instructions
    
    1. Heat the sesame oil in a large pot over medium-high heat.
    Add the garlic, ginger, and scallions to the pot and cook until fragrant, about a minute.
    
    2. Add the enoki mushrooms to the pot and stir-fry for a few minutes, until lightly browned.
    
    3. Pour the vegetable broth into the pot and bring to a boil. Once boiling, reduce heat to medium and simmer for 10 minutes.
    
    4. Add the tofu and miso paste to the pot and stir until the miso paste is dissolved.
    
    5. Simmer for another 5 minutes.
    
    6. Ladle the soup into serving bowls and garnish with additional scallions and sesame seeds, if desired. Enjoy!
