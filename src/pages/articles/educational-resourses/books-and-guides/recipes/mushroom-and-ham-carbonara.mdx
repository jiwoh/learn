# Mushroom and Ham Carbonara
    
    ## Origin and History
    Mushroom and ham carbonara is an Italian classic dish. It gained notoriety in the 1950s when it was served in the Roman trattorias in Italy. Today, it is a popular dish throughout the world, with many variations on the original recipe. The classic version typically includes bacon, mushrooms, eggs, cheese, onion, and black pepper. Often, it is a creamy dish that can be served over various types of pasta.

# Recipe Overview

    **Prep Time:** 20 minutes  
    **Cooking Time:** 25 minutes  
    **Skill Level:** Easy  
    **Spice Level Scoville Rating:** 0-2,000 SHU  
    
    You will need a skillet, a large pot, a colander, and a wooden spoon to prepare Mushroom and ham carbonara.

# Ingredients

## Mushroom and Ham Carbonara Ingredients
    
    - 8 oz. uncooked spaghetti
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
    - 1/2 cup diced ham
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 2 eggs
    - 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    - 1/4 cup heavy cream
    - Salt and pepper, to taste
    - Chopped fresh parsley, for garnish (optional)

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Ham Carbonara
    
    1. In a medium saucepan, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat.
    2. Add 8 ounces of diced bacon and cook until crispy, about 7 minutes; remove from pan and set aside.
    3. Add 1 tablespoon of olive oil to the same pan used for the bacon and heat over medium heat.
    4. Add 1 pound of sliced mushrooms and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
    5. Add 1/4 teaspoon of salt and 1/4 teaspoon of pepper; stir to combine.
    6. Add the bacon back to the pan and stir to combine.
    7. In a large pot, bring 4 quarts of salted water to a boil.
    8. Add 1 pound of spaghetti and cook until al dente, about 8 minutes.
    9. Drain the pasta and return it to the pot.
    10. Add the mushroom and bacon mixture to the pot with the pasta and stir to combine.
    11. In a medium bowl, whisk together 4 eggs, 1 cup of grated Parmesan cheese, and 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper.
    12. Add the egg mixture to the pot with the pasta and stir to combine.
    13. Serve the carbonara warm. Enjoy!
