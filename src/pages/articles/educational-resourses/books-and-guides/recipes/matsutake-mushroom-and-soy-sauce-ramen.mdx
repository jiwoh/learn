# Matsutake Mushroom and Soy Sauce Ramen
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Matsutake Mushroom and Soy Sauce Ramen is a traditional Japanese comfort food dish often enjoyed during the autumn season. Typically made with a broth of soy sauce and shiitake mushrooms, it can also be combined with maitake mushrooms, konbu seaweed, and bonito flakes. The combination of flavors is accompanied by thick noodles, and is usually topped with sliced onions, bean sprouts and pickled ginger.

# Ingredients

- 1 package of dried ramen noodles 
    - 1/4 cup of soy sauce 
    - 1 tablespoon of sesame oil 
    - 2 tablespoons of mirin 
    - 2 tablespoons of sake 
    - 2 tablespoons of sugar 
    - 1/4 teaspoon of salt 
    - Matsutake mushrooms 
    - 4-5 cups of water 
    - 1 scallion, chopped 
    - 1 sheet of roasted seaweed, crumbled 
    - Dollop of hot chili oil (optional)

# Instructions

# Matsutake Mushroom and Soy Sauce Ramen Instructions
    
    1. Heat 3 cups of chicken broth in a pot over medium heat until it starts to boil.
    
    2. In a separate pan, sauté 1/4 cup of sliced mushrooms in 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil for about 3 minutes.
    
    3. Add the mushrooms to the boiling broth, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 10 minutes.
    
    4. Add 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, 1 teaspoon of sesame oil, and 1/2 teaspoon of white pepper to the pot and stir.
    
    5. In a separate pan, add 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil and sauté 1/4 cup of sliced onions for about 2 minutes.
    
    6. Add 2 cloves of minced garlic, 1 teaspoon of grated ginger, and 1/4 cup of sliced mushrooms to the pan and sauté for another 2 minutes.
    
    7. Add the sautéed mixture to the simmering broth and continue to simmer for another 5 minutes.
    
    8. Rinse 2 servings of ramen noodles under cold water and add them to the pot.
    
    9. Turn the heat up to medium and cook the noodles until they are al dente, approximately 5 minutes.
    
    10. Divide the noodles and broth between two bowls, top with the sautéed mushrooms, onions, garlic and ginger, and serve.
