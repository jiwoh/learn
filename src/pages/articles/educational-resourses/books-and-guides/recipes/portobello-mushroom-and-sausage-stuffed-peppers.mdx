# Portobello Mushroom and Sausage Stuffed Peppers
    
    ## Origins and History
    
    This classic dish of stuffed peppers dates back centuries to the first recorded recipes of tomatoes and bell peppers stuffed with meats. Today the classic is made with a stuffing combination of portobello mushrooms, sausage and seasonings that offers a flavorful and filling meal.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Portobello Mushroom and Sausage Stuffed Peppers is 30 minutes, the Cooking Time is 40 minutes, the Skill Level is Moderate and the Spice Level Scoville rating is Medium. You will need a cutting board, paring knife, oven-safe baking dish, spoon, spatula and measuring cup to prepare your Portobello Mushroom and Sausage Stuffed Peppers.

# Ingredients

### Ingredients
    
    - 4 large bell peppers
    - 1/2 pound Italian sausage 
    - 1/2 cup diced onion 
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced 
    - 4 portobello mushrooms, chopped 
    - 1/2 cup cooked long-grain rice 
    - 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese 
    - 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese 
    - 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano 
    - 1/4 teaspoon dried basil 
    - 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
    - 1/4 cup tomato sauce 
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil

# Instructions

## Portobello Mushroom and Sausage Stuffed Peppers Instructions
    1. Preheat oven to 375Â°F.
    2. Grease a shallow baking dish with olive oil and set aside.
    3. In a medium skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat.
    4. Add 1/2 lb of ground sausage and brown until cooked through.
    5. Add 1/2 cup of diced onion and 1/2 cup of diced bell pepper and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
    6. Add 1 clove of minced garlic and 1 cup of sliced portobello mushrooms and cook for 2 minutes.
    7. Stir in 2 tablespoons of tomato paste and 1 teaspoon of Italian seasoning or your favorite herbs.
    8. Remove skillet from heat and season with salt to taste.
    9. Cut 4 bell peppers in half lengthwise and remove the ribs and seeds.
    10. Arrange the peppers in the prepared baking dish.
    11. Divide the sausage mixture among the peppers, stuffing them full.
    12. Top with 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese.
    13. Bake for 25 minutes, or until peppers are tender.
    14. Serve warm. Enjoy!
