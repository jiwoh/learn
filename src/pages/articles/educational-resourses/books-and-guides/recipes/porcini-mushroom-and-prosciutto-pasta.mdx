## Introduction
    
    Porcini mushroom and prosciutto pasta is a dish made from combining two delicious ingredients – porcini mushrooms and prosciutto. This pasta dish is hearty and flavorful with rich umami flavors from the mushrooms and salty notes from the prosciutto. The dish is easy to make and requires minimal ingredients, making it an ideal weeknight meal. 
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Porcini mushrooms have been a part of Italian cuisine for centuries and are native to many parts of Europe. Prosciutto, on the other hand, has been a staple in Italian cuisine since the Roman Empire and has been enjoyed around the world for generations. The combination of porcini mushrooms and prosciutto has likely been enjoyed by Italians since the dawn of time. # Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Porcini Mushroom and Prosciutto Pasta is 10 minutes, the Cooking Time is 20 minutes, the Skill Level is Moderate, and Spice Level Scoville rating is 500. You will need a pot, a skillet, a cutting board, a knife, and a colander to prepare Porcini Mushroom and Prosciutto Pasta.

# Ingredients

- Porcini mushrooms, ¼ ounce, dried
    - Prosciutto, 2 ounces, cubed
    - Extra-virgin olive oil, 2 tablespoons
    - Garlic, 2 cloves, minced
    - Shallots, 2 tablespoons, minced 
    - White wine, 1 cup 
    - Freshly grated Parmesan cheese, ½ cup
    - Unsalted butter, 2 tablespoons
    - Chopped parsley, 2 tablespoons 
    - Salt and freshly ground black pepper

# Instructions

## Porcini Mushroom and Prosciutto Pasta Instructions
    
    1. Heat a large pot of lightly salted water to boiling.
    
    2. In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat.
    
    3. Add the prosciutto and cook until crispy, about 5 minutes.
    
    4. Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
    
    5. Add the porcini mushrooms and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
    
    6. Add the cream and reduce the heat to low. Simmer for 10 minutes.
    
    7. Add the drained pasta to the boiling water and cook until al dente, about 8-10 minutes.
    
    8. Drain the pasta, reserving 1/2 cup of the cooking liquid.
    
    9. Add the pasta to the skillet and toss to combine.
    
    10. Add the reserved cooking liquid and the Parmesan cheese and stir until the cheese has melted.
    
    11. Stir in the parsley.
    
    12. Serve the pasta with freshly cracked pepper, if desired. Enjoy!
