# Mushroom and Goat Cheese Tart 
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom and goat cheese tart is a delicious savory pastry dish that has become popular in recent years. This tart's origins can be traced back to France where it has been served for centuries. The dish is a combination of mushrooms, onions, herbs, and creamy goat cheese baked in a flaky crust. This combination of flavors makes for an irresistible treat that is sure to be a crowd pleaser.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Mushroom and Goat Cheese Tart is 15 minutes, the Cooking Time is 45 minutes, the Skill Level is Moderate, and the Spice Level Scoville rating is 0. You will need a 9-inch tart pan, a rolling pin, a bowl, a pastry brush, and a grater for preparing the Mushroom and Goat Cheese Tart.

# Ingredients

##Mushroom and Goat Cheese Tart Ingredients
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - 1 small onion, diced
    - 4 cloves garlic, minced
    - 8 ounces cremini mushrooms, stemmed and sliced
    - 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves
    - Salt and freshly ground black pepper
    - 8 ounces goat cheese, crumbled
    - 1 sheet frozen puff pastry, thawed
    - 1 large egg, lightly beaten
    - 2 tablespoons chopped flat-leaf parsley

# Instructions

&lt;h2&gt;Mushroom and Goat Cheese Tart Instructions&lt;/h2&gt;
    
    1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C), and lightly butter a 9 inch tart pan.
    
    2. To make the crust, in a food processor, combine the flour and salt, mix briefly to combine.
    
    3. Add the butter and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse meal.
    
    4. Add the egg and cream, and process until a dough forms.
    
    5. Shape the dough into a ball, wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
    
    6. On a lightly floured surface, roll the dough out to fit the tart pan.
    
    7. Place the pastry dough into the tart pan, and trim off any excess dough.
    
    8. Prick the bottom of the pastry dough with a fork several times.
    
    9. Bake the pastry shell for about 20 minutes, or until lightly golden.
    
    10. In a large skillet over medium heat, melt the butter.
    
    11. Saute the mushrooms, onions, and garlic in the melted butter for about 10 minutes, or until lightly browned.
    
    12. Sprinkle the thyme and mushrooms, season with salt and pepper, and stir to combine.
    
    13. In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs, cream, and goat cheese.
    
    14. Pour the egg mixture over the mushrooms and gently stir to combine.
    
    15. Pour the mixture into the pre-baked pastry shell.
    
    16. Bake the tart for about 30 minutes, or until the top is lightly golden and the filling is set. 
    
    17. Allow to cool for 10 minutes before serving.
