# Black Trumpet Mushroom and Asparagus Quiche
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Black trumpet mushrooms and asparagus have long been a favorite combination in French cooking. In this quiche, the two ingredients join forces with cream and Gruyere cheese for a savory, rich pie. This beloved dish dates back to the mid-1800s, when French chefs first began combining their locally-sourced ingredients into a delightful egg-based pie. Quiche has remained a popular dish with home cooks ever since.

# Instructions

## Black Trumpet Mushroom and Asparagus Quiche
     
    ### Ingredients
    - 10 large eggs
    - 2 cups heavy cream
    - 1 1/2 cups shredded gruyere cheese
    - 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
    - 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
    - 1/2 teaspoon salt
    - 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
    - 1/4 cup melted butter
    - 1/2 pound fresh asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1/2-inch pieces 
    - 1/2 pound black trumpet mushrooms, cleaned, stemmed, and sliced
    
    ### Instructions
    1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
    2. In a medium bowl, mix together eggs, cream, cheese, white pepper, nutmeg, and salt.
    3. In a separate bowl, mix together flour and melted butter. Press the mixture into the bottom and sides of a 9-inch deep-dish pie plate.
    4. Arrange asparagus and mushrooms in the bottom of the crust. Pour the egg mixture over the top.
    5. Bake for 45 minutes in the preheated oven, or until the center is set and lightly browned. Let cool for 10 minutes before serving. Enjoy!
