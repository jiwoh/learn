# Mushroom and Chicken Potstickers
    
    Originating in China, potstickers are delicious snacks, appetizers, or even full meals. Traditionally, they consist of a filling of minced meat and vegetables enclosed in a thin, unleavened dough, then steamed or fried. The most popular filling is pork, but other variations include chicken, shrimp, and vegetables. Mushroom and chicken potstickers are a popular combination and offer a hearty, flavorful filling with a crisp exterior.

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Chicken Potstickers
    
    ### Ingredients:
    - 1 package of chicken and mushroom potstickers
    - 2 tablespoons of sesame oil
    - 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
    - 1 tablespoon of grated garlic
    - 1 teaspoon of grated fresh ginger
    - 2 tablespoons of rice vinegar
    
    ### Instructions:
    1. Heat a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat and add the sesame oil.
    2. Add the potstickers and cook for 4 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    3. Once the potstickers are golden brown on the bottom, add the soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and rice vinegar.
    4. Reduce the heat to low and let the potstickers simmer gently, stirring occasionally, for 10 minutes.
    5. Serve the potstickers with your favorite side dish. Enjoy!
