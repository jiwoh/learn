# Mushroom and Onion Focaccia
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom and Onion Focaccia is a delicious Italian flatbread made with a combination of mushrooms, onions, olive oil and herbs. Originating in the Liguria region of Northern Italy, it is believed that focaccia has been made since the Roman times. In modern cooking, focaccia has become a popular appetizer and side dish, often served with a dipping sauce or as a sandwich wrap.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Mushroom and Onion Focaccia is 20 minutes, the Cooking Time is 25 minutes, the Skill Level is Easy to Moderate, and the Spice Level Scoville rating is 0.
    
    To prepare Mushroom and Onion Focaccia, you will need a large bowl, a cutting board, a knife, measuring spoons, and a baking sheet.

# Ingredients

- 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - 1 teaspoon salt
    - 1/2 teaspoon sugar
    - 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
    - 2/3 cup warm water
    - 2 tablespoons minced garlic
    - 2 teaspoons Italian seasoning
    - 2 tablespoons chopped basil
    - 1 onion, sliced
    - 2 cups sliced mushrooms

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Onion Focaccia Instructions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit
    2. Grease a baking sheet or line it with parchment paper
    3. In a large bowl, mix together 2 3/4 cup all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of sea salt, 1 teaspoon of sugar, 1/2 teaspoon of yeast, and 1 1/3 cups of warm water
    3. Knead dough until it forms a ball, about 5 minutes
    4. Place the dough on the baking sheet, flatten it out, and spread it to all edges of the baking sheet
    5. Let the dough rise for about 20 minutes
    6. Make indentations with your fingers to dimple the surface of the dough
    7. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a skillet
    8. Add 1/2 pound mushrooms, 1 chopped onion, 1 teaspoon of garlic and 1 teaspoon of oregano
    9. Cook ingredients until onions are translucent, about 5 minutes
    10. Spread cooked ingredients evenly over the surface of the dough
    11. Bake for about 20 minutes, or until the focaccia is golden
    12. Let cool and enjoy!
