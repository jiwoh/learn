## Origin and History of Enoki Mushroom and Pork Stir-fry
    
    Enoki Mushroom and Pork Stir-fry is an Asian-style dish that first originated in China. It is a simple stir-fry dish with pork slices cooked with enoki mushrooms and other seasonings. The dish has since become popular in other Asian countries including Japan, Korea, and Taiwan, where the enoki mushroom is widely available. It has become a popular way to add flavor to both pork and vegetables, making it a great option for an easy weeknight dinner.

# Instructions

## Enoki Mushroom and Pork Stir-fry Instructions
    
    1. Cut 8 ounces of pork tenderloin into thin slices.
    2. Heat a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
    3. Add 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil. 
    4. When the oil is hot, add the pork slices and stir-fry for about 3-4 minutes until lightly browned.
    5. Remove the pork from the wok/skillet and set aside.
    6. Reheat the wok/skillet over medium-high heat and add another 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil.
    7. When the oil is hot, add 1/2 teaspoon of grated ginger, 2 garlic cloves (minced), and 1/2 teaspoon of red pepper flakes.
    8. Stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
    9. Add 1/2 pound of Enoki mushrooms (ends trimmed off, washed, and dried).
    10. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes.
    11. Add 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, and 1 teaspoon of sugar.
    12. Stir-fry for another 1-2 minutes until the mushrooms are cooked through.
    13. Add the pork back into the wok/skillet and mix everything together.
    14. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
    15. Serve hot with cooked rice.
