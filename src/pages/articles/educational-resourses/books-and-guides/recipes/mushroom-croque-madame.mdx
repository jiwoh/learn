# Mushroom Croque-Madame
    
    ## Origin and History
    Mushroom Croque-Madame is a French take on the classic Croque-Madame sandwich. This version of the sandwich was created in the 1940s in Paris, France as a vegetarian-friendly alternative to the original sandwich. It is traditionally made with a creamy white sauce and fresh mushrooms, and served on toasted bread with a layer of melted cheese.

# Instructions

## Ingredients 
    - 4 ounces thinly sliced mushrooms 
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil 
    - 4 tablespoons unsalted butter 
    - 6 slices of French bread 
    - 1/2 cup grated Gruyère cheese 
    - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley 
    - 4 large eggs 
    - Salt and pepper to taste 
    
    ## Directions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
    
    2. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the mushrooms and sauté for 5 minutes or until golden brown.
    
    3. Butter one side of each slice of bread and place on a baking sheet. Top with the sautéed mushrooms and grated cheese. Bake for 8-10 minutes or until the cheese is melted and the bread is golden brown.
    
    4. Remove from the oven and top with the remaining slices of bread. Place the baking sheet back in the oven and bake for an additional 5 minutes.
    
    5. Meanwhile, melt 1 tablespoon of butter in a skillet over medium heat. Crack the eggs into the skillet and cook until the whites are set, 2-3 minutes.
    
    6. Remove the croque-madame from the oven, top with the eggs and sprinkle with chopped parsley. Serve warm.
