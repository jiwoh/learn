# Mushroom and Brie Tartlets
    
    ## Origin and History
    Mushroom and Brie Tartlets are a savory bite-sized dish filled with sautéed mushrooms, melted Brie cheese, and creamy onions. This luxurious combination first appeared on dinner tables in the late seventies in France and quickly spread across Europe. Today, these tartlets can be found in restaurants around the world and enjoyed at dinner parties and potlucks.

# Recipe Overview

The Prep Time for Mushroom and Brie Tartlets is about 25 minutes, the Cooking Time is about 15 minutes, the Skill Level is easy, and the Spice Level Scoville rating is 0. To prepare Mushroom and Brie Tartlets, you will need baking pans, measuring cups, measuring spoons, a mixing bowl, ets.

# Ingredients

### Ingredients
    - **6** tartlet shells
    - **4** tablespoons butter
    - **4** cups mushrooms, sliced
    - **3** cloves garlic, minced
    - **2** tablespoons parsley, chopped
    - **1** teaspoon thyme, chopped
    - **1** teaspoon olive oil
    - **2** tablespoons shallots, minced
    - **8** ounces brie, cubed
    - **1/2** cup Parmesan cheese, grated
    - **Salt** and pepper, to taste

# Instructions

# Mushroom and Brie Tartlets 
    ## Instructions
    1. Preheat oven to 350°F (176°C).
    2. Grease a mini muffin tin with butter or non-stick spray.
    3. In a large bowl, gently mix together mushrooms, brie, onion, garlic, thyme, and pepper until evenly combined.
    4. Working with one at a time, lay out a sheet of puff pastry, then cut out 2-inch (5 cm) circles with a biscuit cutter or the rim of a glass.
    5. Place each circle in the muffin tin.
    6. Divide the mushroom mixture between the circles and press down lightly with your fingers.
    7. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes, or until golden brown.
    8. Remove from oven and allow to cool slightly before serving. Enjoy!
