# Mushroom and Crab Bisque
    
    ## Origin and History
    Mushroom and Crab Bisque is a classic French soup that dates back to the 19th century. It is traditionally a creamy soup made with a blend of sherry, mushrooms, butter, cream, and a variety of seasonings. Some versions add bits of cooked crab or lobster for a unique flavor. The soup is served hot or cold and is often garnished with parsley, chives, and other herbs.

# Recipe Overview

    This Mushroom and crab bisque recipe has a prep time of 15 minutes and cooking time of 30 minutes, making it a recipe suitable for novice cooks. It has a mild spiciness level with a Scoville rating of 500-2,000. To prepare this recipe you will need a large saucepan, a wooden spoon, a blender and a sieve.

# Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons butter
    - 1/4 cup diced onion
    - 1/4 cup diced celery
    - 1 cup diced mushrooms
    - 2 tablespoons flour
    - 2 cups chicken broth
    - 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
    - 1/2 teaspoon celery seed
    - 1 (8 ounce) can lump crabmeat, drained and flaked
    - 1 (14.5 ounce) can diced tomatoes
    - 1/4 cup dry sherry
    - 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
    - 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
    - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

# Instructions

1. Heat a large skillet over medium heat.
    2. Add 3 tablespoons of butter and melt.
    3. Add 1 chopped white onion to the skillet and cook until soft, stirring occasionally, about 5 minutes.
    4. Add 2 cloves of minced garlic and cook for another minute.
    5. Add 3/4 pound of sliced mushrooms and cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    6. Sprinkle 1 teaspoon of dried thyme, 1 teaspoon of dried tarragon, and 2 teaspoons of salt over the mushrooms, stirring to mix completely.
    7. Add 1/4 cup of brandy and cook until the liquid is almost completely reduced.
    8. Add 3 cups of chicken broth and bring to a boil.
    9. Reduce heat to a simmer and cook for 15 minutes.
    10. Add one 6-ounce can of lump crabmeat, drained, and simmer for another 5 minutes. 
    11. Pour the soup into a blender and process until smooth.
    12. Return the soup to the skillet and add 2 tablespoons of cream. 
    13. Simmer for 2 minutes and serve.
