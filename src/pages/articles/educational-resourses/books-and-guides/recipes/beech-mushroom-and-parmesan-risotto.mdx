# Beech Mushroom and Parmesan Risotto
    
    ## Origin and History
    Beech Mushroom and Parmesan Risotto is a classic Italian dish made with Arborio rice, beech mushrooms, Parmesan cheese and aromatic vegetables. Beech mushrooms are a type of mushroom native to the forests of Europe and North America and have a unique flavor and a firm texture. It is believed that Parmesan Risotto was first created in the 18th century in the region of Piedmont, Italy, and has since become a popular Italian dish.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Beech Mushroom and Parmesan Risotto is 10 minutes, the Cooking Time is 30 minutes, the Skill Level is Beginner, and the Spice Level Scoville rating is 0.
    
    To make the Beech Mushroom and Parmesan Risotto, you’ll need a saucepan, a wooden spoon, a skillet, a colander, and a grater.

# Ingredients

## Beech Mushroom and Parmesan Risotto Ingredients
    * 1 tablespoon olive oil
    * 1 tablespoon butter
    * 1 small onion, chopped
    * 1 cup Arborio rice
    * 4 cups vegetable broth
    * 1 cup white wine
    * 8 ounces beech mushrooms, sliced
    * 1⁄2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    * Salt and pepper to taste

# Instructions

# Beech Mushroom and Parmesan Risotto Instructions
    
    1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
    
    2. Add diced onions and garlic and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
    
    3. Add risotto rice and stir to combine.
    
    4. Pour in white wine and stir until absorbed, about 1 minute.
    
    5. Add the beech mushrooms and stir.
    
    6. Slowly add the warm vegetable stock, about 1 cup at a time, stirring continuously.
    
    7. When the rice is cooked through and creamy, about 25 minutes, fold in chopped parsley and grated Parmesan cheese.
    
    8. Serve risotto warm. Enjoy!
