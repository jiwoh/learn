# Mushroom Pho
    
    Mushroom pho is a vegetarian version of the classic Vietnamese dish pho. It is a flavorful and comforting soup made with hearty mushrooms, fragrant spices, and a broth that has been simmered for hours. The result is a savory and nourishing meal that is perfect for a cold winter's night.
    
    ## Origin and History of Mushroom Pho
    
    Mushroom pho was created in Vietnam in the 1980s and has since become popular in other parts of the world. The dish is believed to be an adaptation of the classic beef pho, but with a vegetarian twist. The addition of mushrooms provides a subtle umami flavor and additional texture to the soup. While its origin is unmistakably Vietnamese, mushroom pho can be enjoyed by people of all cultures and dietary backgrounds.

# Recipe Overview

    - Prep Time for Mushroom Pho: 25 minutes 
    - Cooking Time for Mushroom Pho: 10 minutes 
    - Skill Level for Mushroom Pho: Moderate 
    - Spice Level Scoville rating for Mushroom Pho: Mild 
    
    The basic cooking utensils needed for preparing Mushroom Pho are a pot, knife, cutting board and strainer.

# Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil 
    - 1 large onion, chopped 
    - 1 bunch scallions, chopped
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 4 ounces shiitake mushrooms, stems removed and thinly sliced
    - 4 ounces cremini mushrooms, stems removed and thinly sliced
    - 8 cups vegetable broth
    - 2 tablespoons fish sauce
    - 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
    - 2 teaspoons fresh grated ginger
    - 2 teaspoons sriracha
    - 8 ounces rice noodles
    - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
    - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil
    - 2 tablespoons chopped emerald onions
    - Fresh lime juice, for serving

# Instructions

## Mushroom Pho Instructions
    1. Heat 1 teaspoon of sesame oil in a large pot over medium-high heat.
    2. Add 8 ounces of shiitake mushrooms and cook, stirring occasionally, until browned, about 4 minutes.
    3. Add 4 cloves garlic and 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, and cook, stirring occasionally, until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
    4. Stir in 3 cups vegetable broth and 2 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce.
    5. Increase heat to high and bring to a boil.
    6. Reduce heat to medium-low and add 1 cinnamon stick, 1/4 teaspoon whole cloves, 1/4 teaspoon ground coriander, and 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom. Simmer for 10 minutes.
    7. Add 8 ounces of rice noodles, and cook until tender, about 5 minutes.
    8. Stir in 1 tablespoon of brown sugar, until dissolved.
    9. Taste and adjust flavor as needed, adding more soy sauce, brown sugar, or spices.
    10. Divide noodles into serving bowls and top each bowl with the mushroom mixture.
    11. Serve hot with lime wedges, bean sprouts, chopped scallions, and cilantro. Enjoy!
