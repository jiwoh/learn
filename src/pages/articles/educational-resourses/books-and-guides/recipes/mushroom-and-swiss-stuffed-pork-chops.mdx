# Mushroom and Swiss Stuffed Pork Chops
    
    Origin and History of Mushroom and Swiss Stuffed Pork Chops: This dish has its roots in classic comfort food. It is believed to have originated in the United States in the 1950s. The combination of melted Swiss cheese, sautéed mushrooms, and pork chops has remained a favorite of diners and home cooks alike. This hearty dish is a great way to dress up a simple pork chop for a weeknight meal or special occasion.

# Recipe Overview

The Prep Time for Mushroom and Swiss stuffed pork chops is 20 minutes, the Cooking Time is 30 minutes, and the Skill Level is easy. The Spice Level Scoville rating for this dish is 1.
    
    For preparing Mushroom and Swiss stuffed pork chops, you will need a skillet, measuring cup, cutting board, knife, baking pan and aluminum foil.

# Ingredients

#####Ingredients:
    - 4 (1-inch-thick) boneless pork chops
    - 1/2 teaspoon chopped fresh thyme
    - 1 tablespoon butter
    - 1/2 cup chopped mushrooms
    - 1/4 cup diced onion
    - 2 cloves garlic, chopped
    - 1/2 cup finely diced Swiss cheese
    - 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
    - 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
    - Salt and pepper, to taste
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Swiss Stuffed Pork Chops Instructions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
    
    2. In a medium skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat.
    
    3. Add 1/2 cup of finely chopped onion, 1/2 cup of finely chopped mushrooms, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt and cook until the vegetables are tender, about 5 minutes.
    
    4. Remove the vegetables from the pan and set aside.
    
    5. Place 6 boneless pork chops between two sheets of plastic wrap and pound them with a meat mallet until they are 1/4-inch thick.
    
    6. Divide the vegetables among the pork chops and top each with a slice of Swiss cheese.
    
    7. Roll up each pork chop from the short end and secure with toothpicks.
    
    8. Place the rolls in a lightly greased 9-by-13-inch baking dish.
    
    9. In a small bowl, combine 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard, 1 tablespoon of honey, and 1 tablespoon of olive oil; brush over the rolls.
    
    10. Bake for 20 minutes or until the pork is cooked through.
    
    11. Serve with your favorite side dish. Enjoy!
