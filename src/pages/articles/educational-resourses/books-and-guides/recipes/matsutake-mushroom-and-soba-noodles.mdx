# Ingredients

## Ingredients
    - 8 ounces dried soba noodles
    - 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    - 6 ounces Matsutake mushrooms or other wild mushrooms, sliced
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    - 2 tablespoons mirin
    - 2 teaspoons sesame oil
    - 2 emerald onions, thinly sliced
    - 2 tablespoons toasted sesame seeds (optional)

# Instructions

#Instructions for Making Matsutake Mushroom and Soba Noodles 
    
    1. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
    
    2. Place the soba noodles in the boiling water and cook for 4 to 5 minutes, or according to package instructions.
    
    3. Drain the noodles and rinse with cold water.
    
    4. Heat a large skillet over medium heat and add 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil.
    
    5. Add the sliced matsutake mushrooms to the skillet and sauté until the mushrooms start to turn golden brown, about 3 to 4 minutes.
    
    6. In a medium bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, mirin, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and honey.
    
    7. Pour the sauce into the skillet and continue to sauté until the mushrooms are tender and the sauce is thick, about 2 minutes.
    
    8. Add the soba noodles to the skillet and toss with the mushrooms and sauce.
    
    9. Serve warm, topped with sesame seeds and emerald onions. Enjoy!
