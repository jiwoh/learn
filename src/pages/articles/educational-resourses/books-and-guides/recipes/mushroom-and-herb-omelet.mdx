# Mushroom and Herb Omelet 
    
    ## Origin and History 
    Mushroom and herb omelets have a rich history and have been popular in different parts of the world for centuries. In the Middle East, egg and mushroom is a traditional combination, and in France and western Europe, omelets are a staple dish. Combining mushrooms and herbs in an omelet is a classic way to enjoy the flavors of mushrooms and herbs and has been enjoyed for hundreds of years.

# Recipe Overview

    Mushroom and herb omelet has a prep time of 5 minutes, a cooking time of 5 minutes, an easy skill level and no spice level. You will need a medium bowl, a fork, a non-stick pan and a spatula to prepare the Mushroom and herb omelet.

# Ingredients

- 4 large eggs
    - 1 handful of chopped mushrooms
    - 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh herbs (such as parsley, chives, or tarragon)
    - 1 teaspoon of butter or olive oil
    - Salt and pepper to taste

# Instructions

# Mushroom and Herb Omelet Instructions
    
    1. Crack 2 eggs into a medium bowl and whisk until the yolks and whites are well combined.
    
    2. Heat 1 tablespoon of butter or oil in a nonstick skillet over medium-high heat.
    
    3. Add 1/2 cup of sliced mushrooms and cook, stirring occasionally, until they are browned and softened, about 4 minutes.
    
    4. Add 1 tablespoon of finely chopped fresh herbs, such as parsley, basil, chives, thyme, sage, oregano, or marjoram. 
    
    5. Pour the egg mixture into the skillet and reduce the heat to medium.
    
    6. Let the omelet cook until the underside is light golden brown, about 4 minutes.
    
    7. Carefully flip the omelet and cook until the other side is golden brown and the egg is cooked through, about 3 minutes.
    
    8. Serve the omelet with a sprinkle of freshly grated cheese and a dollop of sour cream or Greek yogurt. Enjoy!
