# Mushroom and Potato Gratin
    
    ## Origin and History
    Mushroom and Potato Gratin is a classic French dish, dating back to the 19th century. Initially, it was a simple combination of potatoes, mushrooms and cream, but over the years variations have emerged, including the addition of herbs, spices, and cheeses. It is an enjoyable, comforting dish perfect for serving at any occasion.

# Recipe Overview

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes  
    **Cooking Time:** 40 minutes  
    **Skill Level:** Intermediate  
    **Spice Level:** Low  
    
    To prepare Mushroom and Potato Gratin, you will need a 9x13 inch baking dish, a large bowl, a cling wrap, a bowl for boiling water, a grater, a sharp knife, a cutting board, and a spoon for stirring.

# Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons butter
    - 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour 
    - 1 teaspoon salt
    - 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
    - 2 cups vegetable broth
    - 1/2 cup heavy cream
    - 2 tablespoons chopped parsley 
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil 
    - 8 ounces sliced mushrooms
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 2 cups cubed, peeled red potatoes
    - 2 cups grated sharp Cheddar cheese

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Potato Gratin Recipe 
    
    1. Preheat oven to 375Â° F.
    2. Lightly grease a 2-quart baking dish.
    3. Peel and slice 2 potatoes into 1/4-inch slices.
    4. Slice 8 ounces of mushrooms (button, shiitake, or a combination).
    5. Mince 2 cloves of garlic.
    6. In a large bowl, toss together the potatoes, mushrooms, garlic, and 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
    7. Sprinkle with salt and pepper, to taste.
    8. Place the potato and mushroom mixture into the prepared baking dish.
    9. Sprinkle with 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese and 1/4 cup of bread crumbs.
    10. Bake for 30 minutes, or until potatoes are tender and golden brown.
    11. Serve warm. Enjoy!
