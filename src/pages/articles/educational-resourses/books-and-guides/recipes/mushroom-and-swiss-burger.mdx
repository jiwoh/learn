    This classic American sandwich is sure to please any fan of mushrooms and hearty Swiss cheese! A delicious combination of flavors, the Mushroom and Swiss burger has been a favorite for decades. 
    
    ## Origin and History of Mushroom and Swiss Burger
    
    The Mushroom and Swiss burger is said to have been created in the 1970s by the McDonald's corporation. It quickly became a popular item on the menu and was eventually adopted by many other fast food restaurants. The sandwich gained further attention when it was featured in a 1990s commercial that featured the catchphrase "Two all-beef patties, special sauce, lettuce, cheese, pickles, onions and mushrooms on a toasted sesame seed bun."

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Swiss burger Instructions
    
    1. Preheat your oven to 375F.
    
    2. Combine 2 tablespoons of butter and 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a small pan and heat over medium heat.
    
    3. Add 1/2 cup of chopped mushrooms and cook until they begin to brown.
    
    4. Add 1 clove of minced garlic and cook for 1 minute.
    
    5. Remove the mushroom mixture from the pan and set aside.
    
    6. Form 2-3 patties from 1 lb of ground beef.
    
    7. Place the patties on a greased baking sheet and season with salt and pepper.
    
    8. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the internal temperature of the patties has reached 160F.
    
    9. While the patties are baking, place 2 slices of Swiss cheese on each patty.
    
    10. Once the patties are done cooking, place them on a bun and top with the cooked mushroom mixture.
    
    11. Serve and enjoy!
