# Portobello Mushroom and Steak Fajitas 
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Portobello Mushroom and Steak Fajitas is a hybrid dish originating from the Tex-Mex cuisine of the United States. The combination of marinated steak and grilled portobello mushrooms has become popular among health-conscious Americans, who enjoy the dish as a tasty and nutritious meal. Traditional fajitas recipes usually feature larger varieties of grilled bell peppers and onions, but the portobello mushroom and steak combination adds an extra earthy flavor that makes this dish unique.

# Instructions

### Portobello Mushroom and Steak Fajitas
    
    #### Ingredients: 
    * 1 lb flank steak
    * 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
    * 1 teaspoon chili powder
    * 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
    * 3 tablespoons olive oil 
    * 2 bell peppers, sliced
    * 2 large portobello mushrooms, sliced
    * 1/2 red onion, sliced
    * 3 cloves garlic, minced
    * 1 tablespoon lime juice
    * 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
    * 8 small flour tortillas
    
    #### Instructions:
    1. In a small bowl, mix together the garlic powder, chili powder, and cumin. Rub the mixture onto the flank steak and set aside.
    2. Heat 1 tablespoon of the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the steak and cook, flipping occasionally, until the steak is cooked to your desired degree of doneness, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the steak from the pan and set aside.
    3. Add the remaining 2 tablespoons of oil to the skillet. Add the bell peppers, mushrooms, and onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for another minute.
    4. Return the steak to the skillet and add the lime juice and cilantro. Stir everything together and season with salt and pepper to taste.
    5. Warm the tortillas in the oven or on the stove top.
    6. Serve the fajitas with the warm tortillas and your favorite toppings. Enjoy!
