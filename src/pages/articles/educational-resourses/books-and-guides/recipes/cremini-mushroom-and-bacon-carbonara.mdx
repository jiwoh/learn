# Cremini Mushroom and Bacon Carbonara 
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Cremini Mushroom and Bacon Carbonara is an Italian-style dish that combines the flavors of smoky bacon, earthy mushrooms, and creamy pasta. It traces its origins back to the town of Amatrice, in the region of Lazio, in the central part of Italy. During the 1950s, the local chefs in Amatrice created Carbonara with the intention of using up the leftover ingredients they had in the kitchen. Over time, the dish spread throughout Italy and is now a beloved favorite worldwide.

# Instructions

## Ingredients
    
    * 8 strips of bacon, diced
    * 4 cloves garlic, minced 
    * 1 cup cremini mushrooms, diced 
    * 4 eggs, lightly beaten
    * 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese, plus more for serving 
    * 1/2 cup heavy cream 
    * Salt
    * Pepper 
    * 8 oz. spaghetti, cooked according to package directions
    
    ## Instructions
    
    1. Heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add bacon and cook until crisp. If there is excess fat, drain and discard.
    2. Add garlic and cremini mushrooms into the skillet with the bacon. Cook until the mushrooms are softened, about 5 minutes.
    3. In a medium sized bowl, whisk together eggs, Parmesan cheese, and cream until everything is well combined. 
    4. Add cooked spaghetti to the skillet with the bacon and mushrooms. Pour the egg mixture into the skillet and stir everything together.
    5. Stir the spaghetti until the egg mixture is absorbed and the pasta is evenly coated, about 2 minutes.
    6. Season with salt and pepper, to taste.
    7. Serve with extra Parmesan cheese, if desired. Enjoy!
