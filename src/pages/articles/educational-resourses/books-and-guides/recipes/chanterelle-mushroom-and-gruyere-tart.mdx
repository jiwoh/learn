    ## Origin and History

The primary ingredient, the chanterelle mushroom, is known to be a gourmet mushroom with a delightful subtle apricot-like flavor. The tart is traditionally served in French and Italian cuisines, but has since evolved to be served across many countries. # Instructions

### Ingredients 
    * 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
    * 1 large onion, chopped
    * 1 1/2 pounds chanterelle mushrooms, cleaned and thinly sliced
    * 1 teaspoon kosher salt
    * 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
    * 1/4 cup dry white wine
    * 1 sheet frozen puff pastry, thawed and rolled out
    * 3/4 cup freshly grated Gruyere
    * 2 tablespoons freshly chopped parsley
    
    ### Instructions
    1. Preheat oven to 400 F.
    2. In a large skillet, heat the butter over medium-high heat and add the onion. 
    3. Cook until the onion is softened and beginning to brown, about 8 minutes. 
    4. Add the mushrooms, salt and pepper and cook, stirring occasionally, until the mushrooms are softened and lightly browned, 8–10 minutes more. 
    5. Add the wine and cook until most of the liquid has evaporated, 1–2 minutes more. 
    6. Place the puff pastry on a baking sheet, prick with a fork and spread the mushroom mixture evenly over the top. 
    7. Sprinkle with the Gruyere and parsley. 
    8. Bake until the pastry is golden brown, about 15–20 minutes. 
    9. Serve warm.
