    Beech mushroom and mushroom gravy is a classic European dish that dates back centuries. It is a simple dish with few ingredients, yet it has a surprisingly complex flavor. This dish is made with beech mushrooms, also known as "buna shimeji," which are often paired with good-quality mushrooms and served in a rich, flavorful gravy. # Instructions

## Beech Mushroom and Mushroom Gravy Instructions
    
    1. Heat a tablespoon of oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
    2. Add 1/2 cup of finely chopped shallots and cook until softened, about 4-5 minutes.
    3. Add 2 cloves of minced garlic, 1 cup of sliced beech mushrooms, and a pinch of salt. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    4. In a small bowl, whisk together 1 cup of vegetable broth, 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour, and 1/4 teaspoon of dried thyme.
    5. Pour the mixture into the skillet, stirring constantly.
    6. Simmer for about 10 minutes until the gravy is thickened and the mushrooms are tender.
    7. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary.
    8. Serve the mushroom gravy over hot cooked rice or mashed potatoes. Enjoy!
