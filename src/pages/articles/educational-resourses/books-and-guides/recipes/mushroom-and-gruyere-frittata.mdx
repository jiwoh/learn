# Mushroom and Gruyere Frittata
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    The origins of the frittata, a traditional Italian omelette, can be traced back to the 16th century. The frittata has since become a popular dish in other parts of the world, such as the United States, where it is often combined with cheese and other ingredients to create unique and flavorful variations. The Mushroom and Gruyere Frittata is one such variation, combining earthy mushrooms, creamy Gruyere cheese, and fresh herbs for a delicious and versatile dish.

# Recipe Overview

The Prep Time for Mushroom and Gruyere Frittata is 10 minutes, the Cooking Time is 45 minutes, the Skill Level for this dish is Intermediate, and the Spice Level is None. For preparing Mushroom and Gruyere Frittata, you'll need the following cooking utensils: a large bowl, a 9-inch baking dish, a large non-stick skillet, and a whisk.

# Ingredients

- **2 tablespoons olive oil**
    - **3/4 cup thinly sliced shallots (about 2 large)**
    - **1/2 pound small cremini mushrooms, sliced**
    - **Salt and freshly ground black pepper**
    - **1/2 cup grated Gruyère**
    - **6 large eggs**
    - **1/4 cup half-and-half**
    - **2 tablespoons chopped fresh chives**

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Gruyere Frittata Instructions
    
    1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    
    2. Heat a 10-inch (26 cm) non-stick, ovenproof skillet over medium heat. Add 2 tablespoons butter and 1 tablespoon olive oil.
    
    3. Add 1 cup (140 g) chopped mushrooms and cook until soft, about 8 minutes.
    
    4. Add 1/4 cup (28 g) diced onion and 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt and cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    
    5. Remove the mushroom mixture from the skillet and set aside in a bowl.
    
    6. Wipe out the skillet, add the remaining 1 tablespoon butter, and set over medium heat.
    
    7. Beat 8 large eggs together with 1/4 teaspoon salt and some freshly ground black pepper.
    
    8. Add the eggs to the skillet and cook, stirring the eggs occasionally, until they begin to set, 3 to 4 minutes.
    
    9. Add the mushroom mixture, 1/2 cup (56 g) grated Gruyère cheese, and 1/4 cup (28 g) diced red bell peppers and stir to combine.
    
    10. Cook for an additional 2 minutes.
    
    11. Bake in the preheated oven until the center of the frittata is set, 10 to 12 minutes.
    
    12. Let cool for 5 minutes. Cut into wedges and serve. Enjoy!
