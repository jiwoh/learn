# Mushroom and Onion Galette 
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom and Onion Galette is a French dish of pastry crust filled with onions and mushrooms. It dates back to the 13th century when French cooks used the traditional pastry to create open-faced pies that combined a variety of savory fillings. Though it was a simple preparation, it was often served at celebratory feasts and occasions as a luxurious dish. Today, it is found on a variety of menus and is enjoyed as an appetizer or a light meal.

# Ingredients

- 1 sheet frozen puff pastry, thawed
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - 2 cups mixed mushrooms, cleaned and sliced 
    - 1 small onion, thinly sliced
    - 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
    - Salt and freshly ground pepper
    - 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese
    - 1 egg, lightly beaten
    - 2 tablespoons finely chopped parsley

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Onion Galette Instructions
    1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. Grease a large baking sheet with butter, set aside.
    3. In a large skillet, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the mushrooms and onions and cook until softened, about 8 minutes.
    4. Add the garlic and thyme and cook for another minute, then season with salt and pepper.
    5. Transfer the mushroom-onion mixture to a plate and set aside.
    6. On a lightly floured surface, roll the puff pastry into a 12-inch (30 cm) round.
    7. Place the round on the prepared baking sheet.
    8. Arrange the mushrooms and onions in the center of the round, leaving a 2-inch (5 cm) border.
    9. Sprinkle the cheese over the mushroom-onion mixture.
    10. Fold up the edges of the pastry over the filling and brush with cream or milk.
    11. Bake until the pastry is golden brown, about 25 minutes.
    12. Enjoy your Mushroom and Onion Galette!
