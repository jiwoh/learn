# Oyster Mushroom and Brie Tart
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Oyster Mushroom and Brie Tart is an elegant French appetizer hailing from the Alsace region of France. It combines pillowy puff pastry with a savory filling of brie, oyster mushrooms and a smattering of herbs – all baked up in a single delicious bite. Oyster mushrooms, with their buttery texture and earthy taste, are a traditional component of the tart, although many variations exist featuring different mushrooms and herbs. The tart is typically served as a starter at dinner parties or as a light lunch, accompanied by a simple salad.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Oyster Mushroom and Brie Tart is 30 minutes, Cooking Time is 30 minutes, Skill Level is Easy and Spice Level Scoville rating is 0, meaning this dish is not spicy. The utensils, pots, pans and etc. needed to prepare Oyster Mushroom and Brie Tart are a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom, a baking sheet, a medium saucepan, a wooden spoon, a whisk, and a rubber spatula.

# Ingredients

## Oyster Mushroom and Brie Tart Ingredients
    
    - 1 sheet frozen puff pastry, thawed
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - 1 small onion, diced
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 8 ounces oyster mushrooms, sliced
    - 2 ounces brie cheese, cubed
    - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
    - 1/4 teaspoon salt
    - 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
    - 2 tablespoons grated parmesan cheese

# Instructions

## Oyster Mushroom and Brie Tart Instructions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 350°F (177°C). Grease a 9-inch tart pan.
    
    2. To make the crust, put the puff pastry sheet on a lightly floured surface and roll it into a 12-inch square. Gently fit the pastry into the tart pan and trim off the excess pastry.
    
    3. Prick the pastry with a fork and place in the refrigerator to chill for at least 15 minutes.
    
    4. Place the tart pan on a baking sheet and bake for 15 minutes.
    
    5. In a large bowl, mix together mushrooms, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, garlic, shallots, salt and pepper until well-combined.
    
    6. Arrange the mushrooms evenly over the pastry base.
    
    7. Slice the Brie into thin slices and arrange over the mushrooms.
    
    8. Bake the tart for 20 minutes, or until the pastry is golden-brown and the cheese is melted.
    
    9. To serve, sprinkle with fresh thyme leaves and drizzle with remaining olive oil. Serve warm. Enjoy!
