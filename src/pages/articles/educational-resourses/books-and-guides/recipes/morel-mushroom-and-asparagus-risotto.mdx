# Morel Mushroom and Asparagus Risotto
    
    ## Origin and History of Morel Mushroom and Asparagus Risotto
    
    Morel Mushroom and Asparagus Risotto is a classic Italian dish that has long been a staple in Italian restaurants. It is a creamy risotto made with a combination of morel mushrooms, white wine, and asparagus. The dish is believed to have originated in Tuscany, Italy, where morels are plentiful. While the dish may have been around for centuries, it only became popular in the United States in the late 1990s. Today, Morel Mushroom and Asparagus Risotto is enjoyed all over the world as a delicious and filling meal.

# Recipe Overview

    ### Morel Mushroom and Asparagus Risotto
    - Prep Time: 20 minutes 
    - Cooking Time: 25 minutes 
    - Skill Level: Intermediate 
    - Spice Level Scoville Rating: 0
    
    This recipe requires a saucepan, large skillet, rice cooker, wooden spoon, and colander to prepare.

# Ingredients

- 1 ounce of morel mushrooms
    - 2 tablespoons of butter
    - 1 small onion, finely chopped
    - 2 cloves of garlic, minced
    - 1 cup of Arborio rice
    - ¾ cup of dry white wine
    - 4 cups of chicken broth
    - ½ cup of grated Parmesan cheese
    - 1 bunch of asparagus, cut into 1” pieces
    - Salt and black pepper, to taste

# Instructions

## Morel Mushroom and Asparagus Risotto Instructions 
    1. Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large saucepan or Dutch oven over medium heat. 
    2. Add 1 cup of chopped shallots and 1 tablespoon of freshly minced garlic to the pot. Sauté for 2 minutes until softened. 
    3. Add 1 ½ cups of risotto rice to the pan and stir to combine with the shallots, garlic, and oil. 
    4. Pour in 2 ½ cups of white wine and simmer, stirring often, until the liquid has almost completely evaporated.
    5. Begin adding 3 ½ cups of vegetable broth one ladleful at a time, stirring after each addition and allowing the liquid to be absorbed before adding more. 
    6. After 15 minutes of adding broth and stirring, add 1 cup of fresh morel mushrooms, 1 bunch of asparagus spears cut into 1-inch pieces, and salt and pepper to taste. 
    7. Continue stirring and adding broth for another 10-15 minutes. 
    8. When the risotto is creamy and the vegetables are just tender, stir in 1 tablespoon of butter and ½ cup of freshly grated Parmesan cheese. 
    9. Serve in bowls and top with extra Parmesan cheese and fresh chopped parsley if desired. Enjoy!
