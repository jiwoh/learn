# Matsutake Mushroom and Rice Pilaf
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Matsutake Mushroom and Rice Pilaf is a popular Japanese dish that has its roots in Japanese culinary traditions. It is made from the highly-prized matsutake mushroom and is usually served with steamed rice. It is a fragrant and flavorful dish that is usually served at special occasions and festivals. The aroma and taste of the matsutake mushrooms can be quite complex and is known to be quite enjoyable.

# Recipe Overview

* Prep time for Matsutake Mushroom and Rice Pilaf is 20 minutes, cooking time is 50 minutes, skill level is moderate, and Spice Level Scoville rating is 0. 
    
    To prepare Matsutake Mushroom and Rice Pilaf, you will need a large pot, a cutting board, a knife, a spoon, and a garlic press.

# Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - 1 small onion, diced
    - 1 large carrot, diced
    - 1 celery stalk, diced
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1 cup long-grain white rice
    - 2 cups chicken broth
    - 1 teaspoon dried thyme
    - 1/2 cup dried Matsutake mushrooms, chopped
    - 2 tablespoons butter, melted
    - Salt and pepper, to taste

# Instructions

## Matsutake Mushroom and Rice Pilaf Instructions
    1. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
    2. Add 1/2 cup diced onion, 1 cup diced carrots, and 2 cloves of minced garlic. Saute for 4 minutes, until the vegetables start to soften.
    3. Add 8 ounces of sliced fresh matsutake mushrooms and 2 teaspoons of fresh thyme. Continue to saute for an additional 4 minutes.
    4. Add 1 cup of Basmati rice and stir to combine.
    5. Pour in 2 cups of chicken stock, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and 1/4 teaspoon of freshly ground pepper.
    6. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let it simmer for 20 minutes.
    7. Remove the lid and fluff the rice with a fork. Serve warm.
