# Chanterelle Mushroom and Truffle Risotto
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Chanterelle Mushroom and Truffle Risotto is a delectably creamy Italian dish made with mushrooms, shallots, white wine, truffle oil and a variety of seasonings. The truffle-infused risotto has been around for centuries in Italy and has become a favorite dish for foodies, chefs and home cooks from all over the world. The chanterelle mushroom is a type of wild mushroom found in forests, fields and meadows — usually during late summer and early autumn. Truffles are an especially choice variety of mushroom, a highly prized delicacy, and the combination of chanterelle mushrooms and truffle oil add an incredible flavour and texture to this classic risotto.

# Recipe Overview

This Chanterelle Mushroom and Truffle Risotto recipe has a Prep Time of 5 minutes and a Cooking Time of 25 minutes. It is considered to be a Moderate level recipe, with a Spice Level of zero on the Scoville scale. To make this dish, you will need a large saucepan, a wooden spoon, some butter, olive oil and a shallow bowl.

# Ingredients

## Chanterelle Mushroom and Truffle Risotto Ingredients
    
    - 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - 1 small onion, chopped
    - 1 cup arborio rice
    - 1/2 cup white wine
    - 4 cups chicken stock or broth
    - 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
    - 1/2 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
    - 2 tablespoons minced fresh chives
    - 1/4 cup diced and sautéed chanterelle mushrooms
    - 2 tablespoons minced truffle
    - Salt and freshly ground pepper, to taste

# Instructions

## Chanterelle Mushroom and Truffle Risotto Instructions
    1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and add 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
    2. Add 1 chopped onion, 1 clove of minced garlic, and 1 teaspoon of dried oregano to the skillet and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
    3. Increase the heat to high and add 1/2 pound of chanterelle mushrooms to the skillet. Cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
    4. Add 1/2 cup of white wine and cook until the liquid has reduced by half, about 3 minutes.
    5. Add 2 cups of Arborio rice to the skillet and stir to coat the grains in the oil.
    6. Add 4-1/2 cups of vegetable or chicken broth gradually, stirring constantly to keep the grains from sticking together.
    7. Simmer for 18-20 minutes, stirring occasionally until the rice is cooked through.
    8. Add 2 tablespoons of butter and 2 tablespoons of freshly grated Parmesan cheese. Stir until the butter has melted and the cheese has melted.
    9. Spoon the risotto onto a serving plate and top with 2 tablespoons of freshly shaved or grated black truffle.
    10. Enjoy!
