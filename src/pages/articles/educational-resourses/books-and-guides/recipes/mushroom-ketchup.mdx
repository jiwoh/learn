# Mushroom Ketchup
    Mushroom Ketchup is an English condiment made from mushrooms, vinegar, spices, and other ingredients. It has been around since the 1700s and was an alternative to the tomato ketchup widely used today. It was also used to flavor and preserve food, and was an important part of English cookery in the 18th and 19th centuries.

## Mushroom Ketchup Instructions
    1. Gather together all the ingredients: 
        - 12 ounces of mushrooms, diced
        - 1 onion, minced
        - 1 garlic clove, minced
        - 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
        - 2 tablespoons of white wine vinegar
        - 1 teaspoon of sugar
        - 2 teaspoons of Worcestershire sauce
        - 2 tablespoons of olive oil
        - Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
    2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
    Add the diced mushrooms and cook until golden, about 5-7 minutes.
    3. Add the onion and garlic, and cook for another 5 minutes until softened.
    4. Add tomato paste, white wine vinegar, sugar, Worcestershire sauce, and season with salt and pepper.
    5. Reduce heat to low and cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    6. Transfer mixture to a blender or food processor and puree until smooth.
    7. Serve as a condiment with your favorite dishes. Enjoy!
