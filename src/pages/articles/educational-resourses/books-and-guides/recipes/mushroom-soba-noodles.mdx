# Mushroom Soba Noodles
    
    ## Origin and History
    Mushroom soba noodles is a popular Japanese noodle dish. The dish originated in the early 1900s when soba noodles were made from buckwheat in the mountain regions of Japan. This dish has since become increasingly popular and is a staple for many Japanese households. The dish combines soba noodles, mushrooms, and other traditional Japanese ingredients such as soy sauce and spring onions. It is a delicious, quick, and nutrient-packed meal.

    To prepare Mushroom Soba Noodles, you will need a wide skillet, pot, strainer, knife, cutting board, and measuring cups and spoons.

# Ingredients

- 8 oz. soba noodles
    - 1/4 cup soy sauce
    - 1/4 cup mirin
    - 1/4 cup water
    - 1 tablespoon sugar
    - 4 tablespoons vegetable oil
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1 red bell pepper, diced
    - 1/2 onion, thinly sliced
    - 8 oz. mushrooms, sliced
    - 2 tablespoons sesame seeds
    - 2 spring onions, thinly sliced
    - Salt, to taste

# Instructions

## Mushroom Soba Noodles Recipe Instructions
    
    1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a rolling boil.
    2. Add soba noodles and cook for 7 minutes or until done.
    3. Drain and rinse with cold water.
    4. In a large skillet, heat oil over medium-high heat.
    5. Add mushrooms and garlic and cook for 5 minutes, stirring often.
    6. Add soba noodles, soy sauce, sesame oil and sugar.
    7. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring often.
    8. Add scallions and bell peppers and cook for another 2 minutes.
    9. Serve warm or at room temperature.
