  # Introduction

## Origin and History of King Trumpet Mushroom and Tofu Stir-fry
    
    King trumpet mushrooms, also known as king oyster mushrooms, have a long history of use in Asia, going as far back as 600 B.C. in Japan. Tofu has been a staple in Japan and China since the 7th century. The combination of these two ingredients has long been used in East Asian cuisine, often in a stir-fry. This dish is simple, yet flavorful, with the earthy flavors of the mushrooms balanced by the creamy texture of the tofu.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for King Trumpet Mushroom and Tofu Stir-fry is 10 minutes, Cooking Time is 20 minutes, the Skill Level is Easy and the Spice Level Scoville rating is Mild. To prepare King Trumpet Mushroom and Tofu Stir-fry, you will need a large skillet or wok, chopping board, knife, and measuring spoons.

# Ingredients

- 1 package of extra-firm tofu (16 ounces)
    - 2 tablespoons oil
    - 8 ounces King Trumpet mushrooms, cut into 1/4-inch slices
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    - 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
    - 2 teaspoons toasted sesame oil
    - 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
    - 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
    - 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    - 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
    - 2 tablespoons water
    - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
    - 2 emerald onions, sliced

# Instructions

## King Trumpet Mushroom and Tofu Stir-fry Instructions
    
    1. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
    2. Add the tofu cubes and cook for 4-5 minutes until golden brown.
    3. Add the king trumpet mushrooms and cook for another 2 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    4. Add the garlic and ginger, stirring to combine.
    5. Add the soy sauce, sesame oil, and rice vinegar; stir to combine.
    6. Add the emerald onion and cook for another 2 minutes.
    7. Add the cooked rice and stir to combine.
    8. Add the red pepper flakes and stir to combine.
    9. Serve hot. Enjoy!
