# Mushroom Miso Soup
    Mushroom miso soup is a hearty and flavorful dish that is widely enjoyed throughout Japan and other parts of Asia. It is made by combining miso paste, dashi (Japanese soup stock), mushrooms, and other vegetables to create a savory soup that packs a powerful umami punch. It is believed to have first been created in the 17th century, when miso paste was widely popularized in Japan.

# Ingredients

- 4 cups vegetable stock
    - 1/4 cup white miso paste
    - 1/4 cup diced onion
    - 2 tablespoons sake
    - 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    - 2 tablespoons mirin
    - 2 tablespoons butter
    - 4 ounces shiitake mushrooms, stemmed and sliced
    - 4 ounces oyster mushrooms, sliced
    - 2 scallions, sliced
    - 2 tablespoons toasted sesame oil

# Instructions

## Mushroom Miso Soup Instructions
    1. Heat 2 tablespoons of sesame oil in a soup pot over medium heat. 
    2. Add 1 small chopped onion and 1 crushed garlic clove and saut√© until softened, about 3 minutes.
    3. Increase heat to medium-high and add 2 cups of diced mushrooms. Continue cooking until mushrooms are browned, about 5 minutes. 
    4. Pour in 6 cups of vegetable broth and bring to a boil. 
    5. Reduce heat to low and stir in 3 tablespoons of white miso paste. 
    6. Simmer for 10 minutes.
    7. Add 8 ounces of soft tofu, 1 tablespoon of mirin, 1 teaspoon of grated ginger, and a pinch of salt to the pot. 
    8. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes.
    9. Ladle the soup into 4 bowls. 
    10. Garnish each bowl with chopped emerald onion and sesame seeds, if desired. 
    11. Serve hot. Enjoy!
