# Maitake Mushroom and Potato Gratin
    
    ## Origin and History 
    Maitake Mushroom and Potato Gratin is a classic French dish featuring overlapping layers of thinly-sliced potatoes, Maitake mushrooms, and cream. The recipe originated in the 19th century and is beloved for its flavorful blend of ingredients and its attractive, layered appearance.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Maitake Mushroom and Potato Gratin is 15 minutes, the Cooking Time is 35 minutes, the Skill Level is Easy and there is a Spice Level Scoville rating of 0. 
    For preparing Maitake Mushroom and Potato Gratin, you will need a baking dish, cutting board, knife, spoon, and a large pot.

# Ingredients

## Maitake Mushroom and Potato Gratin Ingredients
    
    - 3 tablespoons olive oil
    - 10 ounces maitake mushrooms, separated into bite-sized pieces
    - 2 large yellow onions, chopped
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 2 teaspoon fresh thyme
    - 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
    - 2 cups vegetable broth
    - 2 large russet potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
    - 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
    - 1 cup fontina cheese, shredded
    - Fresh parsley, for garnish (optional)

# Instructions

---
    
    ## Maitake Mushroom and Potato Gratin Instructions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
    
    2. Grease an 8 inch baking dish with butter and set aside.
    
    3. Heat a large skillet over medium heat and add 2 tablespoons of butter. 
    
    4. Once the butter has melted, add 1 pound of maitake mushrooms and cook until they begin to brown, about 10 minutes. 
    
    5. Remove mushrooms from the skillet and set aside.
    
    6. In a medium bowl, whisk together 2 cups of heavy cream, 1 teaspoon of salt, and ½ teaspoon of pepper. Set aside.
    
    7. In the same skillet, add 1 tablespoon of butter and 1 tablespoon of olive oil. When the butter has melted, add 2 cloves of minced garlic and cook for 1 minute.
    
    8. Add 5 cups of peeled and sliced potatoes and 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh thyme and cook for 8 minutes, or until the potatoes are just beginning to soften.
    
    9. Pour the cream mixture into the skillet and stir to combine.
    
    10. Add the mushrooms and stir to combine.
    
    11. Pour the mixture into the prepared baking dish.
    
    12. Top with ½ cup of grated Parmesan cheese and 1 tablespoon of chopped parsley. 
    
    13. Bake for 25 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and the gratin is golden and bubbly.
    
    14. Let cool for 5 minutes before serving. Enjoy!
