# Mushroom and Spinach Stuffed Chicken Breast 
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom and spinach stuffed chicken breast is a classic comfort food dish that has been enjoyed over the years by families around the world. Originating in the 18th century in the Mediterranean, this stuffed chicken dish has evolved to include a variety of ingredients, such as garlic, onion, and different cheeses. Today, the most popular version of mushroom and spinach stuffed chicken breast is a simple combination of sautéed mushrooms and spinach, wrapped in a chicken breast and then baked in the oven. This delicious dish continues to be a favorite in many different cultures due to the unique combination of flavors and the versatility of ingredients.

# Recipe Overview

*The Prep Time for Mushroom and spinach stuffed chicken breast is 30 minutes, the Cooking Time is 40 minutes, the Skill Level is Moderate and the Spice Level Scoville rating is 5. Utensils required for this dish include a knife, cutting board, two skillets, measuring cups and spoons, and a baking dish.*

# Ingredients

- 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
    - 5 ounces baby spinach
    - 2 tablespoons butter
    - 1/2 cup shredded provolone cheese
    - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
    - Salt and pepper, to taste

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Spinach Stuffed Chicken Breast Instructions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
    
    2. In a small bowl, combine the mushrooms, garlic, spinach, and cheese.
    
    3. Cut a pocket into each chicken breast and stuff with the mushroom mixture.
    
    4. Heat oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat. Add the chicken and cook for about 5 minutes per side, or until lightly browned.
    
    5. Place chicken in a baking dish and bake for 20 minutes, or until cooked through.
    
    6. Serve with a side of your favorite vegetables or grains. Enjoy!
