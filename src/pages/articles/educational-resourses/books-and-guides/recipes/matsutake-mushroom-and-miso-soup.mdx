  # Introduction

## Origin and History of Matsutake Mushroom and Miso Soup
    Matsutake Mushroom and Miso Soup is a Japanese dish that has been enjoyed for centuries. The ingredient combination of delicate matsutake mushrooms and savoury miso create a delicious, healthy and satisfying soup. Traditionally, matsutake mushrooms, which grow in autumn, were regarded as an auspicious food, and miso has been an essential part of Japanese cuisine since the 8th century. Today, Matsutake Mushroom and Miso Soup is a popular Japanese dish that is enjoyed by people of all ages.

# Ingredients

## Ingredients:
    
    - 8 dried matsutake mushrooms 
    - 2 tablespoons white miso paste 
    - 1/2 cup sake 
    - 1/2 cup mirin 
    - 4 cups dashi 
    - 4 tablespoons soy sauce 
    - 2 tablespoons sugar 
    - 2 emerald onions, finely chopped 
    - 2 tablespoons freshly grated ginger 
    - 2 tablespoons toasted sesame oil 
    - Salt, to taste 
    - 4 sheets toasted nori, cut into thin strips

# Instructions

## Matsutake Mushroom and Miso Soup Instructions
    1. Gather the ingredients: 
        - 4 cups vegetable broth 
        - 1/2 ounce dried matsutake mushrooms
        - 1 tablespoon sake 
        - 2 tablespoons white miso
        - 1 tablespoon soy sauce
        - 2 tablespoons mirin
        - 2 tablespoons thinly sliced scallions
    2. Soak the dried mushrooms in 2 cups of warm water for 30 minutes.
    3. Strain the mushrooms, reserving the soaking liquid.
    4. Heat the vegetable broth in a large pot over medium-high heat.
    5. Add the soaked mushrooms, reserved soaking liquid, sake, miso, soy sauce, and mirin to the pot.
    6. Reduce heat to medium and let the soup simmer for 15 minutes.
    7. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with more miso, soy sauce, or mirin, as needed.
    8. Just before serving, top the soup with the scallions.
    9. Serve the soup warm. Enjoy!
