# Enoki Mushroom and Shrimp Stir-fry
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Enoki mushroom and shrimp stir-fry is a traditional Japanese dish that is said to have originated in the early Edo Period (1603-1868). It is a simple yet delicious dish of stir-fried enoki mushrooms and shrimp cooked in a sweet and savory sauce. The combination of the seafood and mushrooms creates a delicious balance of flavors. In recent years, the dish has gained popularity due to its high nutritional value and versatility, as it can be enjoyed alone or paired with other dishes.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Enoki Mushroom and Shrimp Stir-fry is 10 minutes, the Cooking Time is another 10-15 minutes, the Skill Level is easy, and the Spice Level Scoville rating is mild. To make Enoki Mushroom and Shrimp Stir-fry, you will need a wok or large skillet, a large spoon, and a small bowl.

# Enoki Mushroom and Shrimp Stir-fry Instructions
    
    1. In a large bowl, combine 1 tablespoon of cornstarch with 1/4 cup of water. Add 1 pound of shrimp, mix well and let stand for 15 minutes.
    
    2. Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add 1 tablespoon of minced garlic and saut√© for 1 minute. 
    
    3. Add the shrimp to the pan and cook for 2 minutes, stirring often.
    
    4. Add 1/2 cup of julienned carrots and 1/2 cup of julienned bell peppers to the pan and cook for 2 minutes.
    
    5. Add 1/2 pound of enoki mushrooms, 1 teaspoon of ground ginger, 1 teaspoon of sesame oil, and 1 teaspoon of soy sauce to the pan. Cook for 1 minute.
    
    6. Add 3 tablespoons of chopped emerald onions and cook for 1 more minute.
    
    7. Serve the enoki mushroom and shrimp stir-fry over cooked white rice. Enjoy!
