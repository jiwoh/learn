# Mushroom Meatball Subs
    
    Mushroom meatball subs are an Italian-American sandwich that combines Italian-style meatballs, marinara sauce and melted cheese served on a hoagie roll. This delicious sandwich has become a beloved classic around the world, and is a favorite for both adults and kids alike.
    
    ## Origin and History of Mushroom Meatball Subs
    
    Mushroom meatball subs originated in Italian-American cuisine in the United States. Though the exact origin is unknown, the dish is believed to have first appeared around the mid-20th century. It has since become a popular sandwich in pizzerias and Italian restaurants around the world. The traditional recipe consists of savory beef or pork meatballs, marinara sauce, and melted cheese served on a crunchy hoagie roll. Variations on the traditional recipe often include mushrooms, onion, herbs, peppers and garlic.

# Ingredients

- 1 Tbsp olive oil
    - 1 cup finely chopped onion
    - 1 clove garlic, minced
    - 1 lb. white mushrooms, chopped
    - 1/2 cup zesty Italian-style breadcrumbs
    - 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    - 2 Tbsp chopped flat-leaf parsley
    - 3/4 tsp kosher salt
    - 1/4 tsp freshly ground black pepper
    - 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
    - 4 sub rolls
    - 3/4 cup prepared marinara sauce
    - 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese

# Instructions

## Mushroom Meatball Subs Instructions
    1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
    2. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat and sauté 1 chopped onion and 2 cloves of chopped garlic until onion is soft.
    3. Add 8 ounces of sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they are lightly browned, around 8 minutes.
    4. Transfer the mushroom mixture to a large bowl.
    5. Add 1/2 cup of panko breadcrumbs, 1/3 cup of grated Parmesan cheese, 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh parsley, 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh basil, 1 egg, and 1 teaspoon of salt to the mushroom mixture and mix until combined.
    6. Add 1/2 pound of ground beef to the mushroom mixture and mix until combined.
    7. Roll the mixture into 12 meatballs.
    8. Place the meatballs on a baking sheet and bake for 25 minutes.
    9. Meanwhile slice 4 sub rolls and toast them in a toaster oven or under the broiler.
    10. Place the meatballs in the sub rolls.
    11. Top the meatballs with 4 ounces of fresh mozzarella cheese and bake for another 7 minutes until cheese is melted.
    12. Serve the subs with marinara sauce and enjoy!
