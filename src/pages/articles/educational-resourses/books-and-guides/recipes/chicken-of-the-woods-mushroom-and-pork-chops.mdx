## Origin and History of Chicken of the Woods Mushroom and Pork Chops
    
    Chicken of the woods mushrooms, also known as Laetiporus sulphureus, are edible mushrooms found in North America and Europe. Pork chops and Chicken of the Woods mushrooms were popularized as a dish in the 1960s by French chefs in honor of the famous French dish, 'poulet aux bolets'. The flavor combination of pork and mushrooms is a classic French pairing, as the mushrooms are known to bring out the richness of the pork and help enhance its flavor and texture.

# Instructions

## Chicken of the Woods Mushroom and Pork Chops
    
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
    2. Place pork chops on a sheet pan and season them with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
    3. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and add oil.
    4. Add mushrooms and sauté for 5 minutes or until softened.
    5. Reduce heat to low, add butter and garlic, stirring often.
    6. Cook for 2-3 minutes, until garlic is fragrant.
    7. Add chicken broth, heavy cream, and sage to the pan, stirring to combine.
    8. Simmer for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    9. Place pork chops on the prepared sheet pan and top with the mushroom sauce. 
    10. Bake for 25 minutes or until pork chops are cooked through.
    11. Serve and enjoy!
