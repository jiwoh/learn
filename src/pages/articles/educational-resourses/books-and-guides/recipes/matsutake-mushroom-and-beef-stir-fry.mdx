    ## Origin and History
    Matsutake mushroom and beef stir-fry is a traditional Chinese dish that originated in the Sichuan Province. This dish is made with mushrooms and beef that are stir-fried in a savory sauce. The mushrooms imparts a distinct umami flavor that pairs perfectly with the rich beef and delectable sauce. This dish has been enjoyed for years and is a favorite among Chinese food lovers.

# Instructions

### Matsutake Mushroom and Beef Stir-fry 
    
    #### Ingredients
    - 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    - 1 tablespoon rice wine
    - 2 teaspoons sugar
    - 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1/2 teaspoon grated ginger
    - 1/2 pound beef sirloin, thinly sliced
    - 8 ounces matsutake mushrooms, sliced
    - 1 tablespoon sesame oil
    - 1/2 teaspoon salt
    - 2 tablespoons chopped scallions
    
    #### Instructions
    1. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, rice wine and sugar.
    2. Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add 2 tablespoons of the vegetable oil and swirl to coat the pan.
    3. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for about 30 seconds.
    4. Add the beef and stir-fry for 2 minutes, or until lightly browned. 
    5. Add the mushrooms and stir-fry for another 3 minutes.
    6. Pour the soy sauce mixture into the pan and stir-fry for 1 minute more. 
    7. Once the liquid has been absorbed, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil and the sesame oil.
    8. Season with salt and stir-fry for another minute.
    9. Garnish with the chopped scallions and serve.
