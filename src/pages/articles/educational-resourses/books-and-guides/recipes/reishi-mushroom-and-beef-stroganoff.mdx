# Reishi Mushroom and Beef Stroganoff
    
    ## Origin and History 
    
    Reishi Mushroom and Beef Stroganoff is a classic Russian dish consisting of tender beef cubes, mushrooms, and sour cream, slow-cooked and served over egg noodles. The origin of the dish is unknown but its name is derived from Russian Count Pavel Alexandrovich Stroganov, who is credited with its invention in the 19th century. The dish has become popular around the world, with variations that include the addition of reishi mushrooms to the classic recipe. It is now a staple of many cuisines.

# Ingredients

## Reishi Mushroom and Beef Stroganoff Ingredients
    - 1 tablespoon olive oil
    - 1 onion, diced
    - 1 lb. steak, cut into thin strips
    - 3 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1⁄2 cup white wine
    - 8 ounces reishi mushrooms
    - 1⁄2 cup beef broth
    - 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
    - 1 teaspoon paprika
    - 2 tablespoons tomato paste
    - 1⁄2 teaspoon sea salt
    - 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, minced
    - 4 cups cooked egg noodles

# Instructions

## Reishi Mushroom and Beef Stroganoff Instructions
    1. In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon oil over high heat. 
    2. Add 1 pound of beef strips. Season with salt and pepper, to taste. Brown on all sides, about 8 minutes. 
    3. Reduce to medium heat and add 1 red onion, diced. Cook for 5 minutes, until softened. 
    4. Add 4 cloves of minced garlic, 1 teaspoon of paprika, and 3 tablespoons of tomato paste. Stir to combine and cook for 2 minutes.
    5. Add 1 cup of beef broth, 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce, and ½ teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper. Bring to a boil and cook, stirring occasionally, for 8 minutes.
    6. Add 1 cup of sliced reishi mushrooms. Reduce heat to low and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 5 minutes. 
    7. In a separate pot, cook ½ pound of egg noodles according to package instructions. 
    8. When noodles are done, drain and add to beef mixture. Stir to combine. 
    9. Serve stroganoff in individual bowls and top with a dollop of sour cream and fresh parsley, if desired. Enjoy!
