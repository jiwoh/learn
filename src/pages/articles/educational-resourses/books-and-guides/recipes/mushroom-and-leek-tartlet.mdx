# Mushroom and Leek Tartlet
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom and leek tartlet is an iconic French dish originating from the Provence region. It is created from a flaky puff pastry crust filled with sautéed mushrooms and leeks and is often served as an appetizer, a light lunch, or a side dish. The dish has been adopted by many French chefs and has been adapted to a variety of cuisines, including Italian and American.

# Recipe Overview

The Prep Time for Mushroom and leek tartlet is approximately 20 minutes, the Cooking Time is approximately 25 minutes, the Skill Level is Intermediate, and the Spice Level Scoville rating is 0 SHU. You will need a pastry brush, a pastry blender, a rolling pin, a tartlet mold, and a baking sheet to prepare the Mushroom and leek tartlet.

    9. 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Leek Tartlet Instructions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. Heat 1 tablespoon (15 ml) of oil in a medium skillet over medium-high heat.
    3. Sauté the leeks until they are softened, about 5 minutes. 
    4. Add in the mushrooms and cook until they are softened and lightly browned, about 7 minutes. 
    5. Turn off the heat and set aside. 
    6. Grease a 9-inch (23 cm) tart pan with butter and set aside. 
    7. In a large bowl combine the flour, salt, and pepper. 
    8. Using your fingers or a pastry mixer, cut the butter into the flour until it is the size of small peas. 
    9. Add the water a tablespoon at a time and mix until it forms a dough.
    10. Place the dough onto a heavily floured surface and roll out into a circle about 14-18 inches (35-45 cm).
    11. Transfer the pastry dough to the greased tart pan.
    12. Trim off the excess dough and reserve for later use.
    13. Spread the softened mushroom and leek mixture into the pastry dough and spread evenly.
    14. Place the reserved pastry dough on top and using your fingers press the edges of the dough together.
    15. Place the tart pan in the oven and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25-30 minutes, or until the edges are lightly browned.
    16. Allow the tartlet to cool before serving. Enjoy!
