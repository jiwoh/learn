# Mushroom and Chicken Fajitas
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom and chicken fajitas are a modern twist on an old classic. The spicy combination of mushrooms, chicken, peppers, onions, and tomatoes make this dish a popular favorite. Fajitas originates from Mexico, where the word “fajita” means “little strip.” Originally, fajitas were made with steak or skirt steak, but they can now be made with a variety of different proteins, such as chicken, fish, beef, and pork. This dish is typically served with either flour or corn tortillas, as well as a variety of condiments, such as guacamole, salsa, sour cream, and cheese.

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Chicken Fajitas
    
    ### Ingredients
    - 1 tablespoon olive oil
    - 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, sliced
    - 1 large onion, sliced
    - 2 bell peppers, sliced
    - 1/2 cup mushrooms, sliced
    - 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
    - 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
    - 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
    - Salt and pepper, to taste
    - 4-8 flour or corn tortillas
    
    ### Instructions
    1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
    2. Add the chicken, onion, bell peppers, mushrooms, garlic powder, chili powder, cumin, salt and pepper to the skillet. 
    3. Cook, stirring occasionally, until chicken is cooked through and vegetables are tender, about 8-10 minutes.
    4. Heat the tortillas in a skillet or on the grill for a few seconds on each side.
    5. Serve chicken and vegetables in the warm tortillas, garnished with desired condiments and toppings for fajitas. Enjoy!
