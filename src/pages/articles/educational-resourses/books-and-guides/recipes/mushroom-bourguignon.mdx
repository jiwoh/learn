  # Introduction

## Introduction
    Mushroom Bourguignon is a delicious vegetarian take on the classic French beef stew known as beef bourguignon. It’s made with earthy mushrooms, shallots, carrots, and red wine to create a savory and delicious one-pot stew. 
    
    ## Origin and History
    Mushroom Bourguignon is believed to have originated from the French classic beef stew, Beef Bourguignon. The classic dish was first published in the early 1920's by famous French chef, Auguste Escoffier. Mushroom Bourguignon is a vegetarian take on this classic dish, substituting beef with mushrooms. The dish has since become popular among vegetarians due to its savory and filling nature.

# Recipe Overview

    **Mushroom Bourguignon**
    - Prep Time: 30 minutes
    - Cooking Time: 2 hours
    - Skill Level: Moderate
    - Spice Level Scoville Rating: Mild
    
    Preparing Mushroom Bourguignon requires a bowl, Dutch oven, whisk, measuring cups and spoons, cutting board and knife, and stirring spoon.

# Ingredients

###### Ingredients: 
    - 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    - 1¼ pounds cremini mushrooms, halved
    - 1 yellow onion, diced
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 2 tablespoons tomato paste
    - 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
    - 1 cup dry white wine
    - 1 cup vegetable broth
    - 2 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
    - 2 sprigs fresh thyme, plus more for garnish
    - 1 teaspoon minced fresh rosemary
    - 1 bay leaf
    - Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
    - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley leaves

# Instructions

## Mushroom Bourguignon Instructions
    
    1. Heat a large, deep skillet over medium-high heat and add 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
    
    2. Once the oil is hot, add 1 diced onion, 2 minced garlic cloves, and 2 tablespoons of tomato paste. Sauté for about 4 minutes or until the onions begin to soften.
    
    3. Add 1 pound of diced mushrooms and 2 tablespoons of paprika. Sauté for an additional 5 minutes, stirring frequently.
    
    4. Add 1/2 cup of red wine and bring to a simmer. Simmer for about 5 minutes or until the liquid is reduced by half.
    
    5. Add 1 (14.5-ounce) can of diced tomatoes, 1 bay leaf, 2 teaspoons of fresh thyme, and 2 cups of vegetable stock. Bring to a simmer and then reduce heat to medium-low. Simmer for 10 minutes.
    
    6. Add 1 (15-ounce) can of drained and rinsed cannellini beans. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes or until the mushrooms and beans are cooked through.
    
    7. Taste and season with salt and pepper, as needed. Serve with cooked egg noodles or mashed potatoes.
