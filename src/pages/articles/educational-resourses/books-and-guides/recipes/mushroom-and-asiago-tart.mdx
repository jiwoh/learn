# Mushroom and Asiago Tart
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom and Asiago Tart is a savory quiche-like dish originating in the Mediterranean. Originally a staple of Italian cuisine, this dish can be traced back to the Middle Ages. The combination of flavorful mushrooms and nutty Asiago cheese provides a depth of flavor that makes this tart a go-to for special occasions. It's no wonder that this recipe has become popular around the world and continues to be a favorite today.

# Recipe Overview

Prep time for the Mushroom and Asiago Tart is 30 minutes while the cooking time is an additional 25 minutes. This recipe has a low to moderate skill level. The spice level of this tart is mild with a Scoville rating of less than 500. To prepare the Mushroom and Asiago Tart, you will need a 9-inch tart pan, a large sauté pan, a medium saucepan, a rolling pin, a pastry brush and a pastry cutter.

# Ingredients

### Mushroom and Asiago Tart Ingredients
    
    * 1 sheet frozen puff pastry, thawed
    * 2 tablespoons olive oil
    * 2 cloves garlic, finely minced
    * 2 shallots, chopped
    * 8 ounces baby bella mushrooms, sliced
    * 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme
    * 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano
    * 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
    * 1/4 teaspoon salt
    * 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
    * 1/2 cup Asiago cheese, freshly grated
    * 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley leaves

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Asiago Tart Instructions
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. In a bowl, combine 1 large egg and 1/4 cup (60 ml) of heavy cream.
    3. On a clean surface, lay out 1 sheet of ready-made puff pastry.
    4. Using a knife or rolling pin, lightly roll out the puff pastry until it's slightly thinner.
    5. Gently transfer the puff pastry to a 9-inch (23 cm) tart pan.
    6. Prick the base of the pastry with a fork.
    7. Spread a layer of mustard over the base of the pastry.
    8. In a bowl, combine 8 ounces (227 g) of chopped mushrooms and 1/4 cup (60 ml) of finely diced onion.
    9. Sauté the mushrooms and onion in 2 tablespoons (30 ml) of olive oil until the onions are soft and the mushrooms are lightly browned.
    10. Sprinkle 1/2 cup (120 ml) of grated Asiago cheese over the sautéed mushrooms and onion.
    11. Pour the egg and cream mixture over the mushrooms, onion and Asiago cheese.
    12. Bake the tart in preheated oven for 25-30 minutes until the pastry is golden and the filling is set.
    13. Remove from oven and let cool for 10 minutes before serving. Enjoy!
