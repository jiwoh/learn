# Mushroom and crabmeat tartlets
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom and crabmeat tartlets are a classic French recipe that first appeared in the 16th century. This traditional dish was originally served as a main dish but nowadays is usually used as an appetizer. The combination of mushrooms and crabmeat gives it a unique flavor and creamy texture, making it a favorite among French families.

# Recipe Overview

- Prep Time: 20 minutes 
    - Cooking Time: 15 minutes 
    - Skill Level: Easy 
    - Spice Level Scoville rating: N/A
    
    To prepare Mushroom and crabmeat tartlets, you will need a bowl, a whisk, a skillet, a cutting board, and a baking tray.

# Ingredients

## Ingredients
    * 2 puff pastry sheets
    * 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
    * 2 shallots, minced
    * 2 cloves garlic, minced
    * 250g mushrooms, sliced
    * 1/4 cup white wine
    * 110g crabmeat, flaked
    * 120g cream cheese, softened
    * 2 eggs
    * 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
    * 2 tbsp chopped emerald onions
    * Salt and pepper to taste

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Crabmeat Tartlets Recipe
    
    1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
    2. Line a 12-count muffin tin with 12 tartlet shells.
    3. Heat 2 tablespoons of butter in a large skillet over medium heat.
    4. Add 2 cloves of minced garlic and cook for 2 minutes, stirring constantly.
    5. Add 8 ounces of sliced mushrooms and cook for 6 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    6. Add 1/2 teaspoon of salt, 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper, 1/4 teaspoon of paprika and 1/4 teaspoon of dried thyme to the skillet and cook for 1 minute.
    7. Add 1/3 cup of dry white wine and 1/3 cup of heavy cream to the skillet. Cook for 5 minutes or until sauce has thickened slightly.
    8. Remove pan from heat and stir in 8 ounces of cooked crabmeat.
    9. Divide mushroom mixture evenly among tartlet shells.
    10. Bake at 350°F for 20 minutes or until tartlet shells are golden brown.
    11. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve warm. Enjoy!
