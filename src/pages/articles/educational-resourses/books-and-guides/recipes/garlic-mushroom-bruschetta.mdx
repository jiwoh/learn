# Garlic Mushroom Bruschetta
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Garlic Mushroom Bruschetta is a delicious Italian appetizer that originated in the Tuscan region and features freshly chopped mushrooms with garlic and herbs, served on toasted Italian bread. It dates back to the 16th century and is a great way to enjoy a savory, yet light snack.

# Recipe Overview

    - The Prep Time for Garlic Mushroom Bruschetta is 10 minutes, the Cooking Time is 10 minutes, the Skill Level is easy, and the Spice Level is 0 Scoville rating.
    
    You will need a knife, cutting board, bowl, pan, and garlic press to prepare Garlic Mushroom Bruschetta.

# Ingredients

- 1/2 cup of sliced mushrooms
    - 2 teaspoons of finely minced garlic
    - 1/4 cup of olive oil
    - 4 tablespoons of balsamic vinegar
    - 8 slices of french baguette
    - 1/2 cup of diced fresh tomatoes
    - 2 tablespoons of freshly chopped parsley
    - Salt and pepper to taste

# Instructions

# Garlic Mushroom Bruschetta Instructions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
    2. Slice a French baguette into 1/2 inch thick slices. 
    3. Brush the slices with olive oil and sprinkle garlic powder over them.
    4. Place the baguette slices on a baking sheet.
    5. Bake until golden brown and toasted, about 8 minutes.
    6. In a medium skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat until shimmering.
    7. Add 1/2 cup of minced garlic, stirring frequently, until garlic is fragrant and lightly browned, about 2 minutes.
    8. Add 1 pound of sliced mushrooms and cook until golden brown, about 5 minutes. 
    9. Add 2 tablespoons of white wine vinegar and season to taste with salt and pepper.
    10. Cook for about 2 more minutes, stirring frequently.
    11. Place the toasted baguette slices on a serving platter.
    12. Top with the garlic mushroom mixture.
    13. Serve the bruschetta immediately.
