## Origin and History of Lions Mane Mushroom and Vegetable Kebabs 
    
    Lions Mane Mushroom and Vegetable Kebabs have origins in the Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisine. The combination of mushrooms and vegetables is a popular way to get creative with meal planning. This delightful kebab is the perfect way to bring flavor and texture to the dinner table, no matter the occasion. These kebabs are customarily served with traditional flatbreads, or with salads.

# Recipe Overview

    **Prep Time:** 20 minutes 
    
    **Cooking Time:** 10 minutes 
    
    **Skill Level:** Easy 
    
    **Spice Level Scoville Rating:** 0 
    
    You'll need a cutting board, knife, skewer sticks, grill, and oven to prepare the Lions Mane Mushroom and Vegetable Kebabs.

# Ingredients

###Ingredients
    - 2 large Lion's Mane mushrooms, cut into 1 inch pieces
    - 1 large red onion, cut into 1 inch pieces
    - 1 red bell pepper, cut into 1 inch pieces
    - 1 emerald bell pepper, cut into 1 inch pieces
    - 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into 1 inch pieces
    - 1 zucchini, cut into 1 inch pieces
    - 12 metal or wooden skewers
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - Salt and pepper, to taste

# Instructions

## Lions Mane Mushroom and Vegetable Kebabs 
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. In a small bowl, combine 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil and 1 teaspoon each of salt and pepper.
    3. Cut vegetables into 2 inch cubes, including 1 onion, 2 bell peppers (red and yellow), 2 zucchini, and 2-3 carrots.
    4. In a large bowl, toss vegetables with olive oil mixture to coat evenly.
    5. Arrange vegetables on a baking sheet and bake for 15 to 20 minutes, until vegetables are tender and lightly browned.
    6. Meanwhile, slice 2 Lions Mane mushrooms into 1-inch slices.
    7. Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
    8. Add mushrooms and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    9. Add vegetables and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes, stirring to combine.
    10. Thread vegetables and mushrooms onto skewers, alternating ingredients.
    11. Heat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. 
    12. Grill kebabs for 3-4 minutes per side, or until vegetables are lightly charred and mushrooms are lightly browned.
    13. Serve warm. Enjoy!
