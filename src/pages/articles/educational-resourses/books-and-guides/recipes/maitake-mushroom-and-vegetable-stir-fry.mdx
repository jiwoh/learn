# Maitake Mushroom and Vegetable Stir-fry
    
    ## Origin and History
    Maitake Mushroom and Vegetable Stir-fry is a traditional recipe originating in China. It is said to have been invented by Buddhist monks on the mountain of Maitake during the Edo period. It has since become a popular dish among locals and is a staple in many households. The main ingredients of the stir-fry are maitake mushrooms, vegetables, and soy sauce. The vegetables used in the stir-fry can vary according to preference and can include bok choy, carrots, bell peppers, and bean sprouts.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Maitake Mushroom and Vegetable Stir-fry is 10 minutes, Cooking Time is 15 minutes, Skill Level is easy, and Spice Level Scoville rating is mild. To prepare this stir-fry, you will need a large pan or wok, spatula, cutting board and knife.

# Ingredients

## Maitake Mushroom and Vegetable Stir-fry Ingredients
    
    - 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
    - 1/2 large onion, diced
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 4 ounces maitake mushrooms, separated into individual pieces
    - 2 carrots, sliced
    - 1 red bell pepper, diced
    - 1/2 cup vegetable broth
    - 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    - 2 tablespoons mirin
    - 1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil
    - 1 teaspoon freshly grated ginger
    - 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
    - 2 cups cooked jasmine rice
    - 2 tablespoons chopped scallions, for garnish

# Instructions

## Maitake Mushroom and Vegetable Stir-fry Instructions
    
    1. Prepare the vegetables: 
      * Finely chop the garlic.
      * Wash and dice the carrot, red pepper, and onion.
      * Thinly slice the maitake mushrooms.
    
    2. Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat, and add 1 tablespoon of oil.
    
    3. Add the garlic and vegetables, and sauté for 3-4 minutes until vegetables are tender.
    
    4. Push the vegetables to one side of the pan and add the maitake mushrooms. Sauté for an additional 2-3 minutes.
    
    5. Add the soy sauce and stir to combine.
    
    6. Reduce heat and simmer for 3-4 minutes until the flavors combine.
    
    7. Serve immediately over steamed rice or noodles. Enjoy!
