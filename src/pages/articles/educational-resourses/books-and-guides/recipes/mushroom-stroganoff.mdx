# Mushroom Stroganoff 
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom Stroganoff is a comforting and savory dish of mushrooms in a creamy sauce, believed to have originated in Russia in the 19th century. This dish was named after Count Pavel Alexandrovich Stroganov, a Russian gourmet who was very fond of it. The traditional recipe includes onions, butter, sour cream and mushrooms, but other vegetables such as bell peppers, carrots and celery may be used.

# Recipe Overview

    * Prep Time for Mushroom Stroganoff: 10 minutes 
    * Cooking Time for Mushroom Stroganoff: 15-20 minutes 
    * Skill Level for Mushroom Stroganoff: Easy 
    * Spice Level Scoville rating for Mushroom Stroganoff: 0
    
    To prepare Mushroom Stroganoff, you will need a knife, cutting board, stove top, large skillet, wooden spoon, large pot, and measuring spoons.

# Ingredients

## Mushroom Stroganoff Ingredients
    - 6 Tablespoons Butter
    - 2 cups sliced mushrooms
    - 1 shallot, minced
    - 1 clove garlic, minced
    - 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
    - 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
    - Salt and freshly ground pepper
    - 3/4 cup beef broth
    - 1/2 cup sour cream
    - 2 teaspoons chopped fresh parsley
    - 8 ounces wide egg noodles, cooked

# Instructions

## Mushroom Stroganoff Instructions 
    1. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large, deep skillet over medium-high heat. 
    2. Add 1/2 pound of sliced mushrooms, 1/2 an onion diced, 3 cloves of minced garlic, 1/2 teaspoon of fresh thyme and 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika to the pan. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the mushrooms and onions are soft, about 10 minutes.
    3. Pour in 1/2 cup of dry sherry or white wine and cook until the liquid is almost evaporated, about 5 minutes.
    4. Add 1/2 cup of vegetable broth and 2 tablespoons of tomato paste. Simmer until the sauce is slightly thickened, about 5 minutes. 
    5. Stir in 1/4 cup of sour cream and 1 teaspoon of Dijon mustard and bring the mixture to a simmer. Cook for 1 minute, stirring occasionally. 
    6. Divide the stroganoff among 4 plates and garnish with fresh parsley. Serve with egg noodles or mashed potatoes. Enjoy!
