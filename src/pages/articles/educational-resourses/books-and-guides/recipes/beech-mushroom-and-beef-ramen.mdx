    ## Origin and History
    Beech Mushroom and Beef Ramen is a popular Japanese noodle dish and involves a rich, flavorful broth with fried beef, and beech mushroom. This dish originated in the 1950s, when the first instant ramen noodles became popular in Japan. Since then, it has become a beloved dish, enjoyed across the country and the world.

# Instructions

## Beech Mushroom and Beef Ramen
    
    ### Ingredients
    * 4 cups beef broth
    * 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    * 2 tablespoons mirin
    * 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
    * 4 ounces beef, thinly sliced
    * 4 ounces beech mushrooms
    * 2 tablespoons sesame oil
    * 2 servings instant ramen
    * 2 scallions, thinly sliced
    
    ### Instructions
    
    1. In a medium-sized pot, combine beef broth, soy sauce, mirin, and ginger. Bring to a boil.
    
    2. Once boiling, add the beef and beech mushrooms. Reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    
    3. Meanwhile, in a separate pot, cook the instant ramen according to package instructions.
    
    4. Once the beef and mushrooms are done, strain out the broth and set aside.
    
    5. Heat sesame oil in a large pan, over medium heat. Add the cooked beef and mushrooms. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring frequently, until lightly browned.
    
    6. Pour the reserved broth over the beef and mushrooms and cook for 1-2 minutes more, until heated through.
    
    7. Divide the cooked ramen noodles between 2 bowls and pour the beef and mushroom mixture over the top.
    
    8. Garnish with scallions and serve. Enjoy!
