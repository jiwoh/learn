# Oyster Mushroom and Crab Cakes
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Oyster mushrooms and crab cakes are a classic dish native to the Chesapeake Bay region of the United States. It originated in Baltimore, Maryland in the early 1800s and has remained popular throughout the years. The dish is typically made with fresh oyster mushrooms, succulent blue crab meat, and spices like cayenne pepper and Old Bay seasoning, resulting in a flavorful and unique combination.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Oyster Mushroom and Crab Cakes is 15 minutes, the Cooking Time is 50 minutes, the Skill Level is Moderate and the Spice Level Scoville rating is 0. 
    For the Oyster Mushroom and Crab Cakes, you will need a large mixing bowl, a grater, a large skillet, a slotted spoon and measuring cups and spoons.

# Ingredients

- 1/2 pound oyster mushrooms, roughly chopped
    - 1/2 pound crab meat, picked over and any cartilage removed
    - 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, finely chopped
    - 2 tablespoons emerald onion, finely chopped
    - 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
    - 3 tablespoons Dijon mustard
    - 1/4 cup fine bread crumbs
    - 2 teaspoons garlic, minced
    - 1/2 teaspoon paprika
    - 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
    - 2 eggs, lightly beaten
    - Salt and pepper, to taste
    - Vegetable oil, for frying

# Instructions

## Oyster Mushroom and Crab Cakes Instructions
    
    1. Preheat the oven to 375°F.
    
    2. Heat a large skillet over medium heat and add 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
    
    3. Once the oil is hot, add 1/2 cup of diced onion, 1/2 cup of diced celery, and 1/2 cup of diced red bell pepper. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until vegetables are softened.
    
    4. Add 1 cup of cooked and shredded oyster mushrooms and 1/4 cup of chopped parsley and sauté for an additional 2 minutes.
    
    5. In a medium bowl, combine 1 pound of cooked and shredded crab meat, the cooked vegetable mixture, 2 eggs, 1/4 cup of mayonnaise, 2 tablespoons of Dijon mustard, 2 tablespoons of Old Bay seasoning, 1/2 cup of Panko breadcrumbs, and 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese.
    
    6. Using your hands, mix the ingredients together until just combined.
    
    7. Form the mixture into 12 patties and place them onto a parchment-lined baking sheet.
    
    8. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes.
    
    9. Remove from the oven and serve warm with your favorite dipping sauce. Enjoy!
