# Mushroom and Spinach Quiche
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom and Spinach Quiche is a classic French dish that has been served since the 19th century. The quiche is a type of tart made with a custard filling of eggs, milk, cream, and cheese, along with a variety of savory toppings such as mushrooms and spinach. It is believed to have originated in the Lorraine region of France, where various versions of this dish have been enjoyed by locals for centuries. Today, the quiche remains a staple of the French cuisine and can be found in restaurants and homes all over the world.

# Ingredients

**Ingredients:**
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - 1/2 onion, chopped
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 12 ounces mushrooms, sliced
    - 2 cups baby spinach
    - 6 eggs
    - 1/2 cup heavy cream
    - 1 teaspoon dried basil
    - 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
    - Salt and pepper to taste
    - 1 9-inch deep-dish unbaked pie crust

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Spinach Quiche Instructions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease a 9-inch pie plate and set aside.
    
    2. Heat oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add mushrooms and sauté for about 6 minutes, until softened and lightly browned. Add spinach and garlic and cook until spinach is wilted.
    
    3. In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, salt, pepper, nutmeg, and cheese. Stir in the mushroom, spinach, and garlic mixture.
    
    4. Pour the egg mixture into the prepared pie plate. Bake in preheated oven for 40-45 minutes, until the quiche is golden brown and a knife inserted in the center comes out clean. Let cool before slicing and serving. Enjoy!
