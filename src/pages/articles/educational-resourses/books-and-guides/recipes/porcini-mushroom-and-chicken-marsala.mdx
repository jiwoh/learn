## Origin and History of Porcini Mushroom and Chicken Marsala
    
    Porcini Mushroom and Chicken Marsala is a classic Italian dish that originates from the Lombardy region of Northern Italy, where marsala wine is produced. It typically consists of chicken, mushrooms, and marsala wine that are lightly browned in a pan and served with a rich, creamy sauce. The addition of porcini mushrooms to the dish adds an earthy and nutty flavor that is considered a delicacy. This dish has been enjoyed for centuries and continues to be popular in Italian cuisine today.

# Ingredients

## Ingredients
    - 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - 4 tablespoons butter
    - 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
    - 8 ounces sliced porcini mushrooms
    - 1/4 cup Marsala wine
    - 1/4 cup chicken broth
    - 1/4 teaspoon salt
    - 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
    - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley

# Instructions

## Porcini Mushroom and Chicken Marsala Instructions
    1. Place 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts in a bowl and season with salt, pepper, garlic powder and paprika.
    2. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and add 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
    3. Once the oil is hot, add the chicken to the skillet and cook for 5-6 minutes per side, or until the chicken is cooked through.
    4. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside on a plate.
    5. Add 1 tablespoon of butter and 1 tablespoon of olive oil to the skillet.
    6. Add 1/2 cup of diced shallots and 1/2 pound of sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the shallots are softened and the mushrooms are golden brown.
    7. Add 1/2 cup of chicken broth, 1/3 cup of Marsala wine and 2 tablespoons of fresh thyme to the skillet and scrape up any brown bits from the bottom of the skillet.
    8. Place the cooked chicken breasts back in the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the chicken is warmed through.
    9. Serve the chicken breasts and mushroom Marsala sauce over cooked pasta or rice. Enjoy!
