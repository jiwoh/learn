# Mushroom and Gruyere Tartlets
    
    ## Origin and History
    Mushroom and Gruyère tartlets are classic hors d’oeuvres that have been served at dinner parties and special occasions for decades. Originating from France, the tartlets have been traditionally filled with mushroom and Gruyère cheese, and seasoned with herbs and spices. Over the years, different variations of the tartlets have been created, with other ingredients being included to change up the flavour profiles.

# Ingredients

    - 1 onion, chopped 
    - 2 garlic cloves, minced 
    - 10 ounces mushrooms, chopped 
    - 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme 
    - Salt and pepper to taste 
    - 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour 
    - 1/2 cup vegetable broth 
    - 2 tablespoons dry white wine 
    - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley 
    - 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard 
    - 1 sheet frozen puff pastry, thawed 
    - 1 cup shredded Gruyere cheese

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Gruyere Tartlets Instructions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 350°F (176°C).
    
    2. Heat 2 tablespoon of butter in a medium skillet over medium heat.
    
    3. Add 1 cup of thinly sliced mushrooms to the skillet and season with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, until mushrooms are golden brown, about 5 minutes.
    
    4. In a medium bowl, whisk together 3 eggs, 1/2 cup of heavy cream and 1/4 teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper.
    
    5. Place 12 (3-inch) tartlet pans on a baking sheet and divide 1 sheet of frozen puff pastry among the pans.
    
    6. Divide mushrooms and 2 ounces of grated Gruyère cheese among the puff pastry tartlet pans, then pour egg mixture over top.
    
    7. Place baking sheet with tartlets in preheated oven and bake until golden brown, about 20 minutes.
    
    8. Cool on a wire rack for 10 minutes before serving. Enjoy!
