## Porcini Mushroom and Parmesan Risotto
    
    Porcini Mushroom and Parmesan Risotto is a classic Italian dish made with arborio rice, fragrant porcini mushrooms, parmesan cheese, and a splash of white wine. This dish originated in the northern regions of Italy, where the porcini mushroom is abundant, and is considered to be a classic Italian comfort food.

# Instructions

## Ingredients
    
    - 1.5 ounces dried porcini mushrooms
    - 4 tablespoons butter 
    - 1 large onion, diced
    - 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
    - 1/2 cup white wine
    - 4 1/2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
    - 1/2 cup grated parmesan cheese
    - Salt and pepper, to taste
    
    ## Instructions
    
    1. Place porcini mushrooms in a bowl and cover with 1 1/2 cups of warm water. Let sit for 10 minutes.
    
    2. Melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onion and saut√© until softened, about 5 minutes. 
    
    3. Add Arborio rice to the skillet and stir until coated with butter, about 2 minutes. 
    
    4. Pour in white wine and stir until fully absorbed, about 2 minutes. 
    
    5. Drain porcini mushrooms, reserving the soaking liquid. Chop the mushrooms and add to the skillet.
    
    6. Add 1 cup of the reserved mushroom liquid and 1 cup of the broth to the skillet. Simmer and stir until the liquid is absorbed, about 5 minutes.
    
    7. Continue to add the remaining broth, 1 cup at a time, stirring until the liquid is absorbed each time. Cook until the rice is al dente, about 20 minutes total.
    
    8. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the parmesan cheese. Taste and season with salt and pepper as desired. 
    
    9. Serve hot. Enjoy!
