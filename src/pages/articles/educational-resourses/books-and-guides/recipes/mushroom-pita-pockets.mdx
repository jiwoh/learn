# Mushroom Pita Pockets
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom Pita Pockets are a classic Middle Eastern dish that have been enjoyed for centuries. They can be traced back to ancient Greece and are thought to have been traditionally prepared with lamb and olive oil. Today, Mushroom Pita Pockets are a favorite among vegetarians, as it combines the savory mushroom filling with warm pita bread for a complete meal.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Mushroom Pita Pockets is 20 minutes, the Cooking Time is 30 minutes, the Skill Level is Moderate, and the Spice Level Scoville rating is 0. To prepare Mushroom Pita Pockets, you will need a cutting board, knife, bowl, frying pan, spatula, and pita pockets.

# Ingredients

- 4 pita breads
    - 4 teaspoons olive oil 
    - 1 small onion, chopped 
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced 
    - 8 ounces baby portobello mushrooms, sliced 
    - 1 teaspoon dried oregano 
    - 1/2 teaspoon salt 
    - 1/4 teaspoon pepper 
    - 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 
    - 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese

# Instructions

# Mushroom Pita Pockets Instructions
    
    1. Preheat the oven to 375Â°F.
    
    2. In a medium skillet over medium heat, heat 1 tablespoon of oil. Add 1 onion and cook until it is lightly browned, about 5 minutes. 
    
    3. Add 1/2 pound of mushrooms and cook until they release their liquid, about 5 minutes more. Season with salt and pepper. 
    
    4. Add 1/4 cup of white wine, cover the pan and reduce the heat to low. Simmer until the liquid is completely evaporated, about 8 minutes.
    
    5. After the mixture has cooled slightly, add 1/4 cup of chopped parsley and mix. 
    
    6. Cut 4 whole-wheat pita pockets in half and place them on an ungreased baking sheet.
    
    7. Put 1/4 cup of the mushroom mixture into each pocket.
    
    8. Bake for 8 to 10 minutes, until the pitas are golden and the mushroom mixture is hot. 
    
    9. Serve hot. Enjoy!
