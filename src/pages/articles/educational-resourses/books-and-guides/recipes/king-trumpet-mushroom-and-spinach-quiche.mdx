# King Trumpet Mushroom and Spinach Quiche
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    King Trumpet Mushroom and Spinach Quiche is a savory dish enjoyed by many around the world. It originated in France and was popularized in the United States in the 1960s. The quiche is made with a pie crust, eggs, cream, and a mix of cheese and vegetables, most notably king trumpet mushrooms and spinach. This flavorful and hearty dish continues to be a favorite among both home cooks and restaurant chefs.

# Recipe Overview

    Preparation time for King Trumpet Mushroom and Spinach Quiche is approximately 15 minutes. Cooking time is approximately 40 minutes. The skill level required is intermediate. The spice level Scoville rating is low.
    
    To prepare King Trumpet Mushroom and Spinach Quiche, you'll need the following utensils: a medium-sized bowl, pie dish, whisk, spatula, and a skillet.

# Ingredients

## Ingredients
    * 4 cups sliced king trumpet mushrooms
    * 2 tablespoons olive oil
    * 2 cloves garlic, minced
    * 1/2 teaspoon salt
    * 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
    * 1 (9-inch) unbaked deep-dish frozen pie crust
    * 2 cups washed, stemmed and chopped fresh spinach
    * 1 (3-ounce) package cream cheese, softened
    * 2 eggs
    * 1 cup milk
    * 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

# Instructions

## Ingredients
    * 500g short crust pastry
    * 4 cups of mixed mushrooms 
    * 2 cups of cubed king trumpet mushrooms
    * 2 cups of spinach leaves 
    * 6 eggs 
    * 1/2 cup of cream
    * 1/2 cup of grated cheese
    * 1 tablespoon of olive oil 
    * Salt and pepper to taste 
    
    ## Instructions 
    1. Preheat the oven to 200Â°C.
    
    2. Roll out the short crust pastry and line a 20 cm pie dish. 
    
    3. Bake the pastry case in the oven for 25 minutes or until it is golden brown. 
    
    4. Heat the olive oil in a frying pan and add the mushrooms. Cook until they are golden brown and season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
    
    5. Place the spinach leaves in the bottom of the pastry case. 
    
    6. Layer the mushrooms over the spinach and sprinkle the cheese over the top.
    
    7. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and cream then pour over the mushrooms and spinach.
    
    8. Bake in the oven for 35 minutes or until the quiche is cooked through and golden on top. 
    
    9. Serve and enjoy!
