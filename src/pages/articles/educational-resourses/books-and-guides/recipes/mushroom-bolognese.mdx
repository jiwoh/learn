# Mushroom Bolognese
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Mushroom bolognese has been around for many years, originating in Italy as a traditional pasta dish. The original version was vegetarian, but more recently it has been adapted to include mushrooms as a healthier, meaty alternative. It is a flavorful and hearty dish that can easily be adapted to fit any diet.

# Recipe Overview

Mushroom Bolognese has a prep time of 15 minutes, a cooking time of 45 minutes, a skill level of intermediate, and a spice level of mild (0-2,500 Scoville Heat Units). To prepare Mushroom Bolognese, you will need a saut√© pan, large pot, knife, cutting board, garlic press, and grater.

# Ingredients

* 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
    * 1 large shallot, minced
    * 2 cloves garlic, minced
    * 2 tablespoons tomato paste
    * 8 ounces cremini mushrooms, sliced
    * Salt and freshly ground black pepper
    * 1/2 cup dry white wine
    * 1 (14.5-ounce) can diced tomatoes
    * 2 tablespoons chopped fresh oregano
    * 1/2 teaspoon sugar
    * 1/4 cup heavy cream
    * 1/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan
    * 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley leaves

# Instructions

### Step 1: Preparing the mushrooms 
    1. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil on medium-high heat. 
    2. Add 2 cloves of minced garlic and cook for 1 minute. 
    3. Add 2 cups of sliced white mushrooms, season with a pinch of salt and pepper and cook until softened, about 7 minutes. 
    4. Remove mushrooms from heat and set aside.
    
    ### Step 2: Making the Bolognese 
    1. In the same pan, add 2 tablespoons of olive oil and heat on medium heat. 
    2. Add 1/2 cup of diced yellow onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
    3. Add 1/2 cup of diced celery and cook for 2 minutes.
    4. Add 1/2 cup of diced carrot, 1 tablespoon of tomato paste, 1 teaspoon of oregano, 1 teaspoon of basil and a pinch of sea salt and black pepper. Stir and cook for three minutes.
    5. Add the cooked mushrooms, 1 can of San Marzano tomato sauce, 1/4 cup of white wine, and 1 tablespoon of coconut sugar. Stir and bring to a simmer.
    6. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 25 minutes. 
    
    ### Step 3: Finishing the Bolognese 
    1. Add 1/2 cup of chopped parsley to the Bolognese, stir and serve. 
    2. Enjoy your Mushroom Bolognese over your favorite pasta!
