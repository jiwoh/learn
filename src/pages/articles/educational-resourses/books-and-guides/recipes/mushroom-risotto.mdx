# Mushroom Risotto
    
    ## Origin and History
    Mushroom Risotto is a classic Italian rice dish, hailing from the northern region of the country. It's made by slowly stirring arborio rice in a flavorful broth and is typically served with mushrooms, herbs and cheese. It has been a favorite dish of Italian home cooks for centuries.

# Recipe Overview

    Prep Time for Mushroom Risotto: 20 Minutes 
    Cooking Time for Mushroom Risotto: 40 Minutes 
    Skill Level for Mushroom Risotto: Intermediate 
    Spice Level Scoville rating for Mushroom Risotto: Mild
    
    To prepare Mushroom Risotto, you will need a large pot, a wooden spoon, a ladle, and a strainer.

# Ingredients

## Mushroom Risotto Ingredients 
    - 1 1/2 cups Arborio Rice 
    - 2 tablespoons butter
    - 2 tablespoons olive Oil
    - 1/2 cup of shallots - finely diced
    - 1 garlic clove - minced
    - 4 cups of low-sodium vegetable stock, or chicken stock (warmed)
    - 8 ounces of mushrooms, sliced
    - 1/2 cup of dry white wine
    - 2 tablespoons of fresh parsley - minced
    - 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
    - Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

# Instructions

# Mushroom Risotto Instructions
    1. Heat a large pot on medium heat.
    2. Add 1 tablespoon of olive oil and 2 tablespoons of butter.
    3. Once the butter melts, add 1 finely chopped onion and cook until softened, about 2 to 3 minutes.
    4. Add 1 tablespoon of garlic and cook for an additional minute.
    5. Add 1 pound of mushrooms and cook until they begin to soften, about 5 minutes.
    6. Add 1 cup of Arborio rice and stir to combine with the other ingredients.
    7. Pour in 4 cups of hot chicken or vegetable stock and stir to combine.
    8. Simmer the mixture, stirring occasionally, until the rice is cooked and the liquid has been absorbed, about 20 minutes.
    9. Taste and season with salt and pepper to taste.
    10. Stir in 1/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese.
    11. Serve with additional Parmesan cheese.
