# Chanterelle Mushroom and Chicken Paella
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Chanterelle Mushroom and Chicken Paella is a classic Spanish dish. The combination of mushrooms and chicken in a paella is believed to have originated in the 19th century in the Valencian region of Spain. This traditional dish is known for its savory flavors and its unique flavor combination. Chanterelle Mushrooms are native to Europe and are known for their fruity and slightly spicy flavor. Chicken is often used as a protein in paella recipes and adds additional depth of flavor to the dish.

# Recipe Overview

    - Prep Time: 15 minutes
    - Cooking Time: 45 minutes
    - Skill Level: Medium
    - Spice Level Scoville rating: Low
    
    To prepare this dish, you will need a large skillet or paella pan, a wooden spoon, a spatula, and measuring cups and spoons.

# Ingredients

##### Chanterelle Mushroom and Chicken Paella Ingredients
    
    - Olive oil
    - 1 large onion, diced
    - 1 red bell pepper, diced
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
    - 2 cups uncooked Spanish rice
    - 4 cups chicken broth
    - 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
    - 1/2 teaspoon fresh ground black pepper
    - 2 cups cooked chicken, diced
    - 1 cup fresh chanterelle mushrooms, chopped
    - 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped (optional)
    - 1 lemon, quartered

# Instructions

## Chanterelle Mushroom and Chicken Paella Instructions
    
    1. Preheat oven to 350°F (177°C).
    
    2. In a large, oven-safe skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat. 
    
    3. Add 8 ounces of thinly sliced Spanish chorizo and cook for 2 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    
    4. Add 2 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken thighs and season with 2 teaspoons of smoked paprika, 1 teaspoon of ground cumin, and salt and pepper to taste. 
    
    5. Cook the chicken for 8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it is lightly browned. 
    
    6. Add 1 chopped onion, 4 minced garlic cloves, 1 red bell pepper and 1 yellow bell pepper and cook for 4 minutes until the vegetables are lightly browned. 
    
    7. Add 1 cup of uncooked Arborio rice and stir to combine.
    
    8. Pour in 8 cups of chicken broth and add 1 teaspoon of saffron. 
    
    9. Bring the mixture to a boil, and then reduce the heat to a simmer. 
    
    10. Add 3 cups of chanterelle mushrooms and stir to combine. 
    
    11. Cover the skillet with a tight fitting lid or aluminum foil, and bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes. 
    
    12. Remove from the oven and stir in 1 cup of frozen peas.
    
    13. Place the skillet back in the oven and bake for an additional 10 minutes until the liquid is absorbed and the rice is cooked.
    
    14. Serve hot and garnish with fresh parsley. Enjoy!
