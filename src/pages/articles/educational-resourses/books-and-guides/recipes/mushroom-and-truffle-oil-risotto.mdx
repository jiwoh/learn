# Mushroom and Truffle Oil Risotto
    
    Risotto is a classic Northern Italian dish which is served as a main course or side dish, and is made using arborio rice. This variation on the classic is made with mushrooms and truffle oil, adding an earthy and luxurious flavor that is sure to impress.
    
    ## Origin and History of Mushroom and Truffle Oil Risotto
    
    Mushroom and truffle oil risotto is believed to have originated in the Piedmont region of Italy in the late 19th century, and soon became a popular dish. The addition of truffle oil is a relatively modern twist on the traditional version, lending an earthy and luxurious flavor to the risotto.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Mushroom and Truffle Oil Risotto is about 15 minutes, and the Cooking Time is about 30 minutes. The Skill Level for this dish is medium and the Spice Level Scoville rating is 0. 
    
    To make Mushroom and Truffle Oil Risotto, you’ll need a large saucepan, a wooden spoon, a measuring cup and measuring spoon, a pan lid, and a bowl.

# Ingredients

## Mushroom and Truffle Oil Risotto Ingredients
    - 1 tablespoon olive oil
    - 5 tablespoons butter
    - 1 small onion, diced
    - 1 clove garlic, minced
    - 1 cup arborio rice
    - 3½ cups chicken stock
    - 2 cups mushrooms, cut into cubes
    - 1 tablespoon white truffle oil
    - Salt and pepper, to taste
    - ½ cup Parmesan cheese, grated

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Truffle Oil Risotto
    
    1. Heat 4 tablespoons of olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat.
    
    2. Add 1 small onion or shallot, diced, and 2 cloves of garlic, minced, and sauté for about 5 minutes, until onion is translucent. 
    
    3. Add 8 ounces of sliced mushrooms and sauté for another 3 minutes, until lightly brown. 
    
    4. Stir in 2 cups of arborio rice and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly. 
    
    5. Pour in 5 cups of chicken broth or vegetable broth, bring to boil, reduce the heat and simmer for about 18-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. 
    
    6. When the rice is al dente, stir in ½ cup of white wine, ½ cup of freshly grated Parmigiano Reggiano cheese and season with salt and freshly ground pepper. 
    
    7. Stir in 1 tablespoon of truffle oil. 
    
    8. Serve the risotto warm, garnished with more Parmigiano Reggiano cheese and freshly chopped parsley. Enjoy!
