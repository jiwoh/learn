# Mushroom and Swiss Cheese Quiche
    
    ## Origin and History
    Mushroom and Swiss Cheese Quiche is believed to have originated in France in the late 1800s. It has become a beloved dish in many countries and continues to be a popular breakfast, brunch, lunch, and dinner item. This quiche is composed of a savory egg custard filling with mushrooms, Swiss cheese and onions, topped with a buttery and flaky pastry crust. The decadent combination of ingredients will delight your tastebuds and provide a memorable eating experience for your guests.

# Ingredients

## Mushroom and Swiss Cheese Quiche Ingredients
    
    - 3 eggs
    - 2 cups half-and-half
    - 1 deep-dish pastry shell
    - 1/2 teaspoon garlic salt 
    - 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
    - 1 cup fresh mushrooms, sliced
    - 1 cup Swiss cheese, shredded
    - 2 Tablespoons freshly chopped parsley

# Instructions

## Mushroom and Swiss Cheese Quiche Instructions
    1. Preheat your oven to 350Â°F.
    2. In a medium bowl, mix together 1 cup of shredded Swiss cheese, 1/2 cup of finely chopped mushrooms, and 1/4 cup of chopped emerald onion.
    3. In a separate bowl, beat together 4 eggs, 1/2 cup of half-and-half, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt.
    4. Grease a 9-inch pie pan with butter.
    5. Place one pre-made piecrust in the bottom of the pan.
    6. Sprinkle the cheese, mushrooms, and emerald onion mixture on top of the crust.
    7. Pour the egg mixture over the top.
    8. Bake in preheated oven for 40 minutes, or until the center is set and the top is golden brown.
    9. Let the quiche sit for 10 minutes before serving. Enjoy!
