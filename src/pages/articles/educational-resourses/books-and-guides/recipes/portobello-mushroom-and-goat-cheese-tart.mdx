# Portobello Mushroom and Goat Cheese Tart
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    This robust and flavorful tart is a delicious variation on the classic quiche and has been a breakfast favorite for years. The combination of savory portobello mushrooms, creamy goat cheese and buttery pastry is sure to please any palate. It is thought that the tart originated in France, where it is known as 'Tarte aux Champignon de Portobello et Chèvre'. It has since spread around the world and is enjoyed by many.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Portobello Mushroom and Goat Cheese Tart is 45 minutes, the Cooking Time is 25 minutes, the Skill Level is Beginner, and the Spice Level Scoville rating is 0. A baking sheet, tart pan, knife, cutting board, bowl, and spoon are needed for preparing the Portobello Mushroom and Goat Cheese Tart.

# Ingredients

## Portobello Mushroom and Goat Cheese Tart Ingredients:
    - 5-6 large portobello mushrooms, stemmed and cleaned
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - 1/2 teaspoon coarse Kosher salt
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
    - 1/2 teaspoon Italian seasoning
    - 9-inch refrigerated deep-dish pie crust
    - 1/2 cup crumbled goat cheese
    - 2 tablespoons finely grated Parmesan cheese

# Instructions

## Portobello Mushroom and Goat Cheese Tart Instructions
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F / 190°C. 
    2. Spread 2 tablespoons of olive oil evenly on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
    3. Place the portobello mushrooms on the baking sheet, top with 2 tablespoons olive oil and season them with salt, pepper and garlic powder. 
    4. Bake for 15 minutes.
    5. In a separate bowl, mix together 4 ounces of softened goat cheese with 2 tablespoons of fresh parsley and 2 Tablespoons of fresh basil. 
    6. Remove the mushrooms from the oven and let cool.
    7. Cut the mushrooms into thin slices and place in the center of a 9 x 9 inch tart pan lined with parchment paper. 
    8. Spread the goat cheese mixture in the center of the mushrooms.
    9. Top the tart with 1/2 cup of shredded cheese. 
    10. Bake for 15 minutes until the cheese has melted. 
    11. Let cool for 5 minutes before serving. Enjoy!
