# Coral Mushroom and Asparagus Quiche
    
    ## Origin and History
    
    Coral Mushroom and Asparagus Quiche is a classic French dish that dates back to the 16th century. This quiche is traditionally made with a combination of egg custard, cream, mushrooms and asparagus. While the original recipe used only mushrooms, the addition of asparagus was popularized in the 19th century and the dish continues to be enjoyed today.

# Instructions

## Coral Mushroom and Asparagus Quiche
    
    ### Ingredients: 
    - 3 tablespoons of unsalted butter 
    - 1 small onion, diced 
    - 1 clove garlic, minced 
    - 1 cup Coral Mushroom, diced 
    - 1 cup asparagus tips 
    - 4 large eggs 
    - 1 cup heavy cream 
    - 1 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese 
    - ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper 
    - A 9 inch partially cooked pastry crust 
    
    ### Instructions:
    
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
    
    2. Heat the butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
    
    3. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until the onion is soft and lightly golden, about 5 minutes.
    
    4. Add the coral mushroom and asparagus, season with salt and pepper, and sauté until the mushroom is tender but not brown, about 3 minutes.
    
    5. Remove the pan from the heat and set aside.
    
    6. In a large bowl, beat the eggs until frothy and add the cream, Parmigiano-Reggiano, and pepper.
    
    7. Stir in the mushroom and asparagus mixture.
    
    8. Pour the egg mixture into the partially cooked pastry crust.
    
    9. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the quiche is golden and a knife inserted in the center comes out clean.
    
    10. Let cool for 10 minutes before serving. Enjoy!
