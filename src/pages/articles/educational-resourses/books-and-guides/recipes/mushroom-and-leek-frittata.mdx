# Mushroom and Leek Frittata
    
    ## Origin and History 
    Mushroom and leek frittata is a classic Italian dish dating back centuries. It is a type of omelette, filled with sautéed mushrooms and leeks, and often other ingredients such as cheese, herbs, and vegetables. It has become popular in many countries as a delicious and easy-to-make meal.

# Recipe Overview

    The Prep Time for Mushroom and Leek Frittata is 15 minutes, the Cooking Time is 40 minutes, the Skill Level is easy, and the Spice Level is 0 on the Scoville rating scale. To prepare Mushroom and Leek Frittata, you'll need a 9- to 10-inch oven-proof skillet, whisk, bowl, cutting board and knife.

# Ingredients

- 2 leeks, finely chopped
    - 1 cup of chopped mushrooms (white or baby bella mushrooms work well)
    - 6 eggs, lightly beaten
    - 2 tablespoons of olive oil
    - 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
    - Salt and pepper, to taste
    - 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh basil, oregano, or thyme (optional)

# Instructions

##Mushroom and Leek Frittata Instructions
    
    1. Preheat your oven to 375°F.
      
    2. Heat a medium-sized oven-proof skillet to medium-high heat and add 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil. 
    
    3. Add 4 ounces of chopped mushrooms, 1 cup of chopped leeks, and ¼ teaspoon of salt. 
    
    4. Cook until the vegetables are tender, about 7-8 minutes. 
    
    5. In a medium bowl, whisk together 8 eggs, ¼ cup of heavy cream, and ¼ cup of parmesan cheese.
    
    6. Add the egg mixture to the skillet with the vegetables, and gently scramble with a spoon to combine.
    
    7. Place the skillet in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes or until the top is golden brown.
    
    8. Cut into wedges and serve while hot. Enjoy!
