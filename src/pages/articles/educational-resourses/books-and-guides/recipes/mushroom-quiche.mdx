# Mushroom Quiche 
    
    ## Origin and History 
    Mushroom quiche is a savory dish which originated in France during the early 20th century. Although the exact origin of the dish is unknown, it is thought to have been created as an alternative to the traditional quiche Lorraine. Mushroom quiche is a variation on the classic quiche, incorporating mushrooms as a main ingredient, along with other vegetables such as onions, peppers, and garlic. The dish is usually served as a main course, or as a side dish to other meals. Over the years, many variations have emerged, incorporating different vegetables, cheeses, meats, and spices.

# Ingredients

* 1 9-inch single-crust pie shell
    * 2 teaspoons butter
    * 2 tablespoons minced onion
    * 6 ounces mushrooms, sliced
    * Salt and pepper
    * 1 ½ cups light cream
    * 3 eggs
    * 1 cup shredded Swiss cheese

# Instructions

# Mushroom Quiche Instructions
    
    1. Preheat your oven to 375° F/ 190°C.
    
    2. Grease an 8-inch (20 cm) deep-dish pie plate.
    
    3. In a large bowl, combine 1 ⅓ cups of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of salt and 3 tablespoons of cold, cubed butter.
    
    4. Mix the ingredients together with a pastry blender until the butter is evenly distributed and the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    
    5. Add 5 tablespoons of cold water and mix until the dough forms a ball.
    
    6. Wrap the ball of dough in plastic wrap and place in the refrigerator for 30 minutes.
    
    7. Remove the dough from the refrigerator and roll it out on a lightly floured surface.
    
    8. Place the dough into the greased pie plate and fold the edges to form a crust.
    
    9. Place 2 tablespoons of butter into a large skillet and turn the heat to medium-high.
    
    10. Add 3 cups of diced mushrooms and sauté for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    
    11. In a medium bowl, whisk together 4 eggs, 1 ½ cups of half-and-half cream, 1 teaspoon of salt and ¼ teaspoon of ground black pepper.
    
    12. Sprinkle 1 cup of grated cheddar cheese over the bottom of the pie crust.
    
    13. Spread the cooked mushrooms over the cheese.
    
    14. Pour the egg mixture over the mushrooms and cheese.
    
    15. Sprinkle another cup of grated cheddar cheese over the top of the quiche.
    
    16. Place the quiche in the preheated oven and bake for 40 minutes.
    
    17. Let the quiche cool for 15 minutes before serving. Enjoy!
