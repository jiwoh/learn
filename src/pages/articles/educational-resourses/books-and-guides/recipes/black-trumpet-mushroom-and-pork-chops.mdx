# Black Trumpet Mushroom and Pork Chops 
    
    ## Origin and History 
    Black trumpet mushrooms, also known as horn of plenty or named after the French phrase trompettes de la mort, are an edible fungi know for their flavor and texture. The pork chop, on the other hand, is a cut of meat taken from the ribs of a pig. Pork chops have been enjoyed since ancient times in many different cultures. Combining the two, black trumpet mushrooms and pork chops, is a classic recipe that is often served as a hearty main dish.

# Instructions

## Black Trumpet Mushroom and Pork Chops Recipe
    
    ### Ingredients
    - 4 boneless pork chops
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil
    - 2 shallots, minced
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves
    - 1/2 cup dry white wine
    - 1/2 cup chicken broth
    - 1/2 pound black trumpet mushrooms (or other wild mushrooms)
    - 2 tablespoons butter
    - Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
    
    ### Instructions
    1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. 
    2. Add the pork chops and cook for about 5 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through. 
    3. Transfer the pork chops to a plate and cover with foil to keep them warm.
    4. Add the shallots, garlic, and thyme to the same skillet and cook for about 1 minute.
    5. Increase the heat to high and add the white wine and chicken broth. Bring to a boil and cook for about 5 minutes, or until the liquid is reduced and syrupy.
    6. Add the mushrooms to the skillet and cook for about 3 minutes, or until the mushrooms are just tender.
    7. Add the butter and stir to combine.
    8. Return the pork chops to the skillet and cook for an additional 2 minutes. 
    9. Season with salt and pepper, to taste.
    10. Serve the pork chops topped with the mushrooms and shallots. Enjoy!
