# Definition
    
    Sympodial is a pattern of growth in some fungi, where a single mycelium produces multiple fruit bodies, typically of the same species, in distinct and successive waves.
    
    # Significance
    
    Sympodial growth is significant to mycology as it is a key feature in the classification of some species of fungi. It is also utilized by mycologists to help determine the identity, distribution, and abundance of species in ecological surveys. Sympodial growth is an important factor in understanding the dynamics of fungal populations in an ecosystem.

Through analysis of Sympodial growth patterns, mycologists are able to gain insight into the genetic control of cell wall formation, and can develop treatments to target related diseases.
