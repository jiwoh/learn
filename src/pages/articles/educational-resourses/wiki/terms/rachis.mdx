## Definition
    
    Rachis is the specialized hyphae found in fungi that are interconnected in a branching system. It is a structural component that contains and supports the spores and other parts of the fungus. 
    
    ## Significance
    
    Rachis is an important part of a fungus's anatomy and is responsible for its sclerotial support, foraging for food, and dispersal of spores, among other functions. It is also integral to the formation of hyphal networks which provide increased surface area for spore production and enhance nutrient uptake throughout the organism. The structure and complexity of the fungal rachis gives the fungus greater ability to cope with environmental conditions and survive.

# Usage

Rachis is an important tool used in mycology to characterize the features of fungal hyphae. It is especially useful for studying the diverse morphologies of the many types of fungi. Rachis can be used to measure parameters such as the septal number, emergence angle, and pattern of branching, which are all essential characteristics in the taxonomy and classification of fungal species. The results obtained from Rachis provide researchers with a quantitative dataset which allows for the comparison between and within different fungal species, aiding taxonomic decisions. Furthermore, this data can be used to identify important evolutionary relationships between species, which can lead to further discoveries in mycology.
