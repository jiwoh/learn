# Invasive Candidiasis
    
    Invasive candidiasis is an infection caused by the genus Candida. It is considered a life-threatening disease that can penetrate the bloodstream and various organs, such as the lungs and brain. If left untreated, it can be fatal. The most common type of invasive candidiasis is caused by Candida albicans, although other species such as Candida glabrata and Candida parapsilosis have also been implicated in cases of invasive candidiasis.
    
    ## Significance of Invasive Candidiasis to Mycology
    
    Invasive candidiasis is of significant importance to mycology because it is one of the most common fungal infections and is a major cause of morbidity and mortality in humans. Furthermore, the pathogenicity of Candida species is determined largely by the species involved and the host's immunity. Thus, mycologists must understand how the different species of Candida cause invasive infections, the host-pathogen interactions that lead to disease, and potential therapeutic targets for treating or preventing the infection.
