## Pedicel
    
    In mycology, a pedicel refers to the sterile stalk that connects a mushroom’s spores to its cap. It is also known as a stipe. A pedicel is one of the principal components of a mushroom’s fruiting body.
    
    ## Significance of Pedicel to Mycology
    
    The pedicel is an important structure in mycology and can provide important taxonomic and identification information. The shape and structure of the pedicel can vary greatly between species, and can help differentiate between different species of mushrooms. Additionally, mushroom spores are often discharged from the pedicel, so an examination of the pedicel can provide insight into a mushroom's spore production and dispersal.
