## Definition
    
    Hymenium is the spore-bearing surface of a fungus. It is typically composed of all the fertile surface layers of a fruiting body (often referred to as a mushroom) including the gills, pores, or ridges, depending on the type of fungus.

    Hymenium is essential to the reproductive cycle of fungi and contains the cells responsible for releasing airborne spores. During sexual reproduction, the hymenium aids in the exchange of genetic material between two compatible mating types of fungi.
