# Wood-decay Fungi
    
    Wood-decay Fungi are a group of fungi which have the ability to degrade and cause decay of the cell walls of woody plants, eventually leading to their death. These fungi are an important part of the natural environment and are responsible for the removal of deadwood and the recycling of nutrients in various ecosystems. Wood-decay fungi have also been utilized by humans throughout history for the production of food, medicines, and defense against decay in ships, buildings and objects.
    
    Wood-decay fungi are often divided into two major groups: white-rot fungi, which break down both lignin and cellulose components of wood, and brown-rot fungi, which primarily degrade only the lignin component. The most common wood-decay fungi are Basidiomycota, which produce basidiospores and produce the characteristic wood-decay fruiting bodies.
