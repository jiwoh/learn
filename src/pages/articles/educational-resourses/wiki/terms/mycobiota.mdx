## Mycobiota
    Mycobiota refers to the total fungal composition of a specific region or environment. This includes all fungi present in the area, including both free-living species and those associated with other organisms, including lichenized fungi, mycorrhizal fungi, and pathogenic species.
    
    ## Significance of Mycobiota
    Mycobiota are an important component of a balanced ecosystem and can have a profound impact on the environment, both directly and indirectly. For example, they are responsible for nutrient cycling and decomposition of organic matter, and can provide an important source of food and medicine. Additionally, they can also play a role in plant and animal health, as well as provide insight into climate change and environmental change. The comprehensive understanding of a region's Mycobiota can help inform decision-making related to conservation and management of natural resources.
