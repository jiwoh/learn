# Definition
    
    A propagule is a unit of reproduction in fungi that is capable of producing a new organism. It can refer to a single spore, a group of hyphae, a fragment of mycelium, or a small piece of tissue from a larger organism.
    
    # Significance
    
    Propagules are an essential part of the life cycle of fungi, allowing them to disperse to new habitats and reproduce offspring. Propagules are especially important for mycelial species since many of these organisms are unable to move on their own. Additionally, propagules can remain dormant until environmental conditions are favorable for growth, allowing the species to survive in challenging habitats.

# Usage

This is especially important in cases of aggressive fungal invasions, as the need for early detection is paramount to limiting the damage caused by the pathogen. Propagules also aid in the evaluation of control measures, offering scientists the opportunity to track the efficacy of preventative steps. Moreover, understanding the germination of propagules can provide insight into the evolutionary trajectory of a species and inform conservation efforts. Through their studies of propagules, mycologists can gain an invaluable understanding of mycoflora dynamics.
