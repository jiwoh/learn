# Definition
    A Basidium is a specialized structure that appears on the surface of a mushroom and produces spores. 
    
    # Significance
    Basidia are of particular importance in mycology, as they are used to identify the species of a mushroom. They are also important in the cultivation of mushrooms, as they are responsible for the initiation of mushroom fruiting. Additionally, basidia are used to measure the health of a mushroom's mycelium, since the amount of spores produced is directly related to the strength of the mycelium.

By studying the shape and sporulation of Basidium, mycologists can accurately identify mushroom species.
