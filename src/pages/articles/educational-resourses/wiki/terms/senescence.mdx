# Senescence
    
    Senescence is the process of aging in plant and fungi tissues as a result of physiological and environmental factors. In mycology, senescence generally refers to the aging and degradation of fungus cells, specifically the hyphae, as a result of their natural life cycle. 
    
    Senescence can be actively caused by environmental factors, such as its exposure to light, temperature, oxygen, and humidity. It can also be attributed to certain aging factors, such as the depletion of cellular energy, a decrease in the photosynthetic abilities of the fungus, or disruptions to its metabolism. 
    
    Senescence is an important part of understanding the life cycles of fungi, particularly in the context of their growth, development, and decomposition in diverse ecosystems. By studying senescence, mycologists can gain insights into the interactions between fungi and their environment, and how they contribute to the larger natural cycle of life.
