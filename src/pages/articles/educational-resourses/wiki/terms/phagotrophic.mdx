# Definition
    
    Phagotrophic (also known as phagotrophy) is a type of nutrition and mode of life in which an organism obtains energy and nutrients by ingesting solid organic particles. This is in contrast to osmotrophy, which involves the absorption of dissolved organic molecules.
    
    # Significance in Mycology
    
    In mycology, a mode of nutrition is often an important factor for classifying organisms. For example, some fungi are obligate phagotrophs, meaning that they cannot survive without ingesting particles. Phagotrophic fungi play an important role in the breakdown of organic matter in ecosystems, a process known as decomposition. Additionally, phagotrophic fungi are often important for medical and industrial applications, such as bioremediation and producing enzymes for fermentation in food production.
