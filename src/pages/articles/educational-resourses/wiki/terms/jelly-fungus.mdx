# Jelly Fungus
    
    Jelly fungus is an umbrella term that encompasses a large group of species of the kingdom Fungi. These fungi have jelly-like or gelatinous bodies, and they range in color from white to yellow to pink. Jelly fungi are saprotrophs, which means they feed on dead or decaying organic material.
    
    # Significance of Jelly Fungus
    
    Jelly fungi are important to mycology because they are one of the most ubiquitous kinds of fungi. They can be found in a wide range of habitats, from deserts to forests to grasslands to aquatic environments. In addition, their jelly-like bodies can help to break down complex carbon molecules, allowing them to recycle nutrients in the environment. Jelly fungi also play an important role in the food web by providing a link between detritivores and producers. Because of their unique abilities, jelly fungi are an important part of any healthy ecosystem.
