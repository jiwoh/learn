# Mycosis
    
    Mycosis is an infection of a host caused by a pathogenic fungus. It can be localized, systemic, or superficial and can include both clinically significant and subclinical infections. Certain species of fungi are known to cause diseases in humans and animals, while others cause disease in plants.
    
    ## Significance of Mycosis in Mycology
    
    Mycology is the scientific study of fungi, and a major component of this is the understanding and diagnosis of mycoses. By studying mycoses, mycologists are able to identify and differentiate between fungal pathogens that can cause disease and those that are harmless. This knowledge is useful not only in diagnosing and treating mycoses, but also in understanding the global ecology or fungi and in developing strategies for their management.
