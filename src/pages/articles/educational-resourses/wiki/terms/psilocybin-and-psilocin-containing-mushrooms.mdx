## Definition
    
    Psilocybin and psilocin-containing mushrooms, also known as magic mushrooms, are fungi of the genus Psilocybe that produce the psychoactive compound psilocybin. The mushrooms usually have a brown or reddish-brown cap with a white to yellowish stem and gills. 
    
    ## Significance to Mycology 
    
    Psilocybin and psilocin-containing mushrooms are an integral part of mycology, due to their psychoactive properties and potential therapeutic benefits. Research over the past several decades has shown that psilocybin and psilocin, produced by these mushrooms, can have many positive effects on the human psychological and physical state, such as reduced anxiety, improved mood, and increased creativity. Studies also suggest that these compounds have potential medical applications, such as being able to treat depression, addiction, and PTSD. Therefore, Psilocybin and psilocin-containing mushrooms have become an important subject of mycology, as they can be studied to understand their effects on the human brain and body.
