## Apical
    
    Apical refers to the apex or tip of a biological organism, such as a mushroom or a fungal hypha. In mycology, the term apical is used to describe the highest growing point of a mushroom, the primary area where energy is focused and directed during its development. The apical area is where new growth emerges and where spores are released, giving the mushroom its unique structure and form. Apical is further associated with the orientation of a mushroom and its ability to navigate its environment, where the apex is used to orient the mushroom towards food sources, light, and other environmental conditions.

# Usage

Apical has become an increasingly important tool in mycology, particularly in research and practical applications. Apical enables mycologists to identify different species of fungi in a more accurate and efficient way than traditional methods. This is achieved by using high-resolution images of the fungi, including the hyphae network and the spore patterns, to identify the species in question. Apical is also useful for identifying the morphology of fungal structures and thus aiding in the classification of new fungi. The ability to accurately identify fungi species has significant scientific and practical applications, such as monitoring the spread of pathogenic fungi and understanding the diversity of fungi in the environment.
