# Definition
    A Racket cell is a type of hypha cell, which are filaments that form the structure of a fungus. It is a specialized cell, whose walls have thickened on one side. Racket cells form club-shaped structures that are used for the distribution and infection of spores.
    
    # Significance
    Racket cells are important for spore dispersal, as the swollen sides act as a lever, allowing the cell to propel a spore up to ten times its own length. They also help to support the fungal hyphae, and to spread the fungus. As a result, they are essential for the successful growth and reproduction of a fungus.
