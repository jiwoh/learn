## Definition: 
    Slime molds, or Mycetozoa, are a taxonomic group of non-animal eukaryotic organisms. These organisms form a colony of individual amoeboid cells that can produce a slimy mass to move around as a single organism.
    
    ## Significance to Mycology:
    Slime molds are considered important to mycology as they provide insight into the evolutionary pathway of fungi. Many researchers believe that slime molds may represent a missing link between protists and fungi, and the study of their molecular and cellular features could provide insight into the evolutionary changes that have taken place during their transition from protists to fungi. In addition, studying the environmental requirements of slime molds may provide insights into the environmental conditions that promote the emergence of other fungi. Additionally, because slime molds can survive in harsh environments, understanding their physiology may lead to new strategies for the cultivation of fungi in hostile environments.

Slime molds are versatile and widely used in mycology for a variety of purposes.
