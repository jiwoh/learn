# Definition
    
    Mantle is a thin outer layer of fungal hyphae that coats the outside of a fruiting body.
    
    # Significance
    
    Mantle plays an important role in the life cycle of a fungus. It is what makes the fungus visible and characterized, and it contributes to the formation of the spores. The presence of a mantle is also a key way to differentiate between different species of fungi. In some species, the mantle also contains pigmentation or melanin, which helps protect the spores from ultraviolet light and other environmental factors.

# Usage

Mantle is widely used in mycology, particularly in the study of fungal genetics. Mantle is used to detect the presence of specific fungal genes, understand the genetic heterogeneity among different fungal species, and monitor the genetic variability within a single species. Mantle is also used to identify polymorphisms, mutations and genetic recombination events, providing insight into the evolutionary history of fungal species. Further, Mantle can be used to construct phylogenetic trees, which helps to classify fungi based on their evolutionary history, aiding in the identification and authentication of fungal species. All of these applications are essential to advancing our understanding of fungal genetics and utilizing it to resolve various practical problems.

# Example

Mantle is a powerful tool used to investigate the diversity of fungi, especially mushrooms. It has been used extensively in research to assess the taxonomic composition, abundance, and distribution of mushrooms found in a given habitat.
