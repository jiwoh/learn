# Definition of Lactophenol cotton blue mount test
    
    Lactophenol cotton blue mount test is a histological technique for the microscopic examination of fungal hyphae, which are the cellular components that make up a fungal colony. This technique uses a mixture of cotton blue, lactophenol, and glycerin, which are then applied to a microscope slide and heated. This allows for the hyphae to be stained and mounted in the slide and viewed under a microscope.
