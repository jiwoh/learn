# Ostiole 
    
    Ostiole (plural: Ostioles) is a specialized, spore-bearing structure that is present in certain species of basidiomycetes, a type of fungus. It is the terminal end of the basidiospore-producing structure, the basidium. The Ostiole contains a pore or opening through which the basidiospores, the reproductive cells of the fungus, are discharged.
    
    ## Significance of Ostiole to Mycology
    
    The Ostiole is an important feature of fungal reproductive anatomy as it marks the point of spore discharge. This knowledge is essential for the identification of fungal species and the classification of fungi into taxonomic groups. Additionally, the presence or absence of an Ostiole is used to determine the classification of a fungus as an agaric, the most common type of basidiomycete.
