## Definition
    
    A Septum is a thin, cross-walled structure that can branch off from the main hyphae in fungal mycelia and serves as a link between cells. It is composed of chitin, an organic compound found in the cell walls of fungi and other organisms.
    
    ## Significance in Mycology
    
    Septa are an important structural element in the study of fungi. They facilitate the process of cell division and the exchange of nutrients and compounds between cells. They are essential in the formation of fungal networks and the regulation of growth and reproduction in fungi. Septa also help maintain the shape and integrity of mycelium and provide some protection against environmental stressors. Septa have been studied and used in biotechnological applications, such as in the use of fungal "hairs" to improve water filtration.
