# Oral Candidiasis
    
    Oral candidiasis, also known as thrush, is a fungal infection of the oral mucosa caused by the opportunistic yeast Candida albicans. It is characterized by white cheesy plaques and/or red patches that form on the mucous membranes of the mouth, lips, and tongue. Common symptoms of oral candidiasis include burning, dryness, and soreness of the affected areas, as well as a changed sense of taste.
    
    ## Significance to Mycology
    
    Oral candidiasis is an important disease in mycology as it is the most common fungal infection to affect humans. As Candida albicans is an opportunistic pathogen, it can cause other systemic diseases, such as esophagitis and endocarditis. Thus, it is essential that mycologists understand the pathogenesis and treatment of this disease in order to provide effective care and improve patient outcomes.

Thus, oral candidiasis is an important tool for mycologists to gain a better understanding of the pathogenicity and epidemiology of C. albicans, while also providing insights into the development of potential treatments and preventions strategies.&lt;/p&gt;
