## Definition
    
    Hyphomycetes are a large and diverse group of fungi typically characterized by the presence of aseptate (non-septate) or lightly septate hyphae. They lack the true hyphal septa that are typically found in other fungal taxa, such as Ascomycetes, Basidiomycetes and Glomeromycetes.
    
    ## Significance to Mycology
    
    Hyphomycetes are an important and diverse group of fungi in many terrestrial and aquatic ecosystems. They are often used as model organisms for mycological research, as most species are readily available in nature and have been studied extensively in the lab. Many species of Hyphomycetes also produce useful parthenocarps (asexual reproductive structures in fungi that produce viable spores without mating) which are important in industry, agriculture, and medicine. Hyphomycetes also act as decomposers, helping to cycle essential nutrients in the environment, making them important components of food webs and nutrient cycle maintenance.
