# Definition
    
    Turgid is a term used to describe the physical state of the cell walls of a fungus, which are swollen and firm as a result of the accumulation of large amounts of intracellular water. In contrast, non-turgid fungal cell walls are limp and lack rigidity.
    
    # Significance to Mycology
    
    The turgid state of fungal cell walls is essential for normal cell growth and development, as it allows them to form tight connections with other fungal cells. This tight intercellular adhesion is necessary for efficient communication between cells and for the successful completion of the various phases of the life cycle. Additionally, turgidity is an important factor in fungal cell resiliency, which allows fungi to survive in extreme conditions and in hostile environments.
