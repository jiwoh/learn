# Definition of Lumen
    
    Lumen is a measurement used in mycology to describe the internal diameter of a mushroom's gills or lamellae. The lumen measurement is used to help identify different species of mushrooms as well as better understand mushroom anatomy.
    
    # Significance of Lumen
    
    The lumen measurement is an important tool for mycologists. As many mushrooms are difficult to identify with the naked eye, this measurement helps to differentiate between species. Additionally, measuring the lumen of a mushroom can be a useful indicator of the species' maturity and provide insight into its health. The internal diameter of a mushroom's gills or lamellae can vary between species, so measuring the lumen accurately can be a useful way to determine the mushroom's identity and its overall health.

Furthermore, measurements of light intensity are also useful for identifying fungal pathogens, as well as providing information on the characteristics of a fungus' environment. Additionally, Lumen measurements can also be used to evaluate the efficacy of different methods used to control fungal growth in vitro.
