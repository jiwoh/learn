# Zoospore
    
    A Zoospore is a motile single-celled reproductive stage of some algae, bacteria and fungi. It has a single, flagellated, haploid (1N) cell that is released from the mother organism due to environmental conditions or other triggers. It is capable of actively swimming and seeking out nutrients, surviving for a brief period before rejoining with another Zoospore, in order to produce a new organism.

    Zoospores are of particular interest to mycology, as they are the primary reproductive stage for many species of fungi. They are capable of dispersing fungal spores over large distances, which is critical for the survival and spread of the species. The ability to disperse zoospores has enabled many species of fungi to survive and thrive for long periods of time, despite their limited reproductive capabilities.
