# Chytridiomycota
    
    Chytridiomycota, also known as chytrids, is a division of fungi comprising a diverse group of organisms commonly found in fresh water, soil, and other moist environments. Chytrid fungi are distinguished from other fungi by their zoosporic mode of reproduction and their ability to produce motile zoospores with a single posterior flagellum.

    Chytridiomycota are of significant importance to mycology due to the fact that they are among the early diversifying fungi. Finally, chytrids are essential for the global cycling of carbon, nitrogen, and other essential elements.
