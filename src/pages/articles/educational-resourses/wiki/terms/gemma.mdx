# Gemma
    
    Gemma (plural: gemmae) is a type of asexual spore produced by some species of fungi, lichens, and mosses. The word is derived from the Latin word for ‘gem’ and refers to the shape of the spore, which often appears as a tiny cup or lens. Gemmae can be spread through a process known as gemmation and and are often dispersed through water or air.

As such, gemmae are a useful tool for researchers and identification of species for taxonomic purposes.
