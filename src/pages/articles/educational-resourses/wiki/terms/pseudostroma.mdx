# Pseudostroma
    
    Pseudostroma is an important component in the mycological classification system used to place different fungi in a broad taxonomic category. Pseudostroma is a suborder of the Phyllomycetales order and is primarily distinguished by its unique chalazal cells in the capillitium. Fungi that are classified under Pseudostroma typically produce spores that are globose or ellipsoidal, smooth and hyaline. They differ from the other suborder of Phyllomycetales, Echinosteliales, as they lack echinulated cells.
