# Definition
    
    Hygroscopy is the ability of a substance to absorb moisture from the surrounding environment. This is a physical property of a material that is encountered in many different areas of industry and research.
    
    # Significance to Mycology
    
    In mycology, the hygroscopic nature of fungi is especially important to consider when dealing with fungal spores, tissue, and other fungal parts. Fungi rely on the ability of their cell walls to preferentially absorb or release water in order to maintain their cellular integrity and health. Additionally, hygroscopy plays a role in the dispersal of fungal spores and the infection of fruiting bodies, in addition to other more specific applications. The hygroscopic nature of fungi also serves as a key indicator of the presence of a fungus, as the visualization of hygroscopic properties is widely used for the rapid identification of fungi. As such, hygroscopy is an essential and widely applicable concept in the study of mycology.

Hygroscopicity can be used to promote fungal growth and development, as well as to protect fungi from adverse conditions such as desiccation and water stress.
