# Definition
    
    Spawn is a mass of mycelium, or rooted filaments, formed by a fungus for the purpose of reproduction.
    
    # Significance
    
    Spawn is a vital part of the life cycle of a fungus, and its formation is essential for the spread of the species. During its formation, the fungus will begin to form reproductive structures such as spores, which can be used to spread to new areas. The formation of spawn also helps the fungus to access new food sources and establish new colonies. Spawn is also the main source of commercial production of edible mushrooms and helps to cultivate them in a controlled environment.

Spawn is an essential tool for mycologists, providing an important substrate for studying and manipulating fungal growth.
