# Polyporaceae
    
    Polyporaceae is a large family of fungi in the order Polyporales. As a family, they are characterized by a variety of morphological features, including fleshy or leathery fruiting bodies with radial or concentric tubes. They are found growing on wood in almost all climates.

    Polyporaceae are important economically, as many species are commercially harvested for their culinary and medicinal uses. Polyporaceae are also a model organism for studying the effects of fungal pathogens in wood-decay systems.
