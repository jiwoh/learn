    Homothallism is a reproductive trait in fungi in which the formation of fruiting bodies (such as mushrooms) requires the mating of genetically compatible haploid nuclei from two individuals of the same or closely related species.     Homothallism is important in mycology as it allows fungi to self-fertilize, enabling them to reproduce more quickly and efficiently.
