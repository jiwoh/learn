# Definition
    
    Diplanetism is a phenomenon observed in some species of fungi, in which two opposing branches arise from the same stalk.
    
    # Significance
    
    Diplanetism is an important characteristic of some species of fungi, as its presence can provide insight into the nature and evolutionary history of the organism. For example, two branches arising on the same stalk could indicate an adaptation towards increased surface area for distributing or receiving various nutrients or gases, or could signify a recent evolutionary split from a common ancestor. As such, it provides a key point of study for mycologists attempting to determine species-level relationships and evolutionary histories.
