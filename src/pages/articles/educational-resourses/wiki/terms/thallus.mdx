# Definition of Thallus
    Thallus is a term used in mycology to refer to a vegetative body or growth of a fungus, composed of a mass of undifferentiated cells. The thallus does not have a differentiated body structure like a root system, stems, or leaves as found in plants. It can take on a variety of forms, from a single celled organism to a complex and differentiated structure.
